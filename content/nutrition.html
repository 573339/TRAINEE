• A.Introduction
    • This is a lesson on Introduction to Nutrition, the intent is to teach the benefits of good nutrition and how nutritional affects your performance.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • DISCUSS guidelines on changing nutritional habits.
    EXPLAIN the Navy Operational Fueling Series (NOFS).
    DISCUSS the five fundamental principles in developing a successful nutrition plan.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Guidelines on Changing Nutrition Habits
      • a.The foods you eat play a large role in how you perform, and how you feel.
      • b.Poor nutrition leads to:
        • (1)Low energy
        • (2)Injury and disease
        • (3)May result in early release from the Navy if body fat standards are not continuously met.
      • c.Simple steps are provided to help develop your own plan to achieve your performance goals.
      • d.If you want to be successful, use your training to establish good habits that you can continue.
    • 2.Navy Operational Fueling Series (NOFS)
      • a.Developed to provide a world class nutrition tool to simplify nutrition guidelines.
      • b.Scientifically based collaboration between Navy dietitians and the Athletic Performance Institute (API).
      • c.Terminology used in this program is exactly what they use when teaching nutrition to professional athletes from all sports.
      • d.Navy teamed up with API to bring this tool to Sailors.
    • 3.Five Fundamental Principles That Assist in Developing a Successful Nutrition Plan
      • a.Eat Clean
        • (1)Come back to Earth -- Choose the least processed foods.
          • (a)“Whole” foods such as:
            • 1)Fruits
            • 2)Vegetables
            • 3)Lean Protein (meat, beans, nuts)
            • 4)Low Fat Milk or Yogurt (non-fat or 1%)
            • 5)Whole grains that include the word “whole” as the first ingredient on the food label.
          • (b)Processed foods are those found at most fast food restaurants and in the freezer section of the grocery store.
          • (c)They do not resemble the food that came from the ground or the animal. Think of a hot dog.
        • (2)Eat Color - Eat a variety of fruits or vegetables daily.
          • (a)Naturally occurring color in food means more nutrients.
          • (b)Try to have at least three (3) colors at each meal, such as:
            • 1)Yellow
            • 2)Orange
            • 3)Green
            • 4)Red
            • 5)White
            • 6)Blue/Purple
          • (c)Make half your plate fruits, vegetables and grains.
          • (d)Artificial color does not count, such as:
            • 1)Candy
            • 2)Food Dye on Icing
        • (3)The less the better - Include lean sources of protein daily.
          • (a)Choose protein from sources without legs.
            • 1)Fish
            • 2)Shrimp
            • 3)Black Eyed Peas
            • 4)Kidney Beans
          • (b)Choose protein from sources with two legs.
            • 1)Chicken
            • 2)Turkey
            • 3)Egg
          • (c)Limit the ones with four legs.
            • 1)Burgers
            • 2)Bacon
            • 3)Pork Chops
            • 4)Hot Dogs
            • 5)Sausage
        • (4)Eat healthy fats.
          • (a)Healthy fats provide energy and keep you feeling full.
          • (b)Choose most of your fat from plant sources.
            • 1)Nuts
            • 2)Oils
            • 3)Seeds
          • (c)Include Omega 3 fatty acids
            • 1)Tuna
            • 2)Salmon
            • 3)Walnuts
            • 4)Flax Seeds
          • (d)Limit fat from animal sources
            • 1)Butter
            • 2)High fat meats (bacon)
            • 3)Fried foods
      • b.Eat Often
        • (1)Make every attempt to Eat breakfast every day, it does not have to be breakfast foods.
        • (2)Choose a lean protein source and some fruit, dairy and/or whole grains.
        • (3)Spread calories out over the day to maintain energy levels and prevent overeating at night.
      • c.Hydrate
        • (1)Stay hydrated.
        • (2)Drink water instead of sugary beverages, such as:
          • (a)Soda
          • (b)Sweet Tea
        • (3)Fruit Beverages, Electrolyte replacement drinks are not needed unless:
          • (a)Exercising for greater than 90 minutes.
          • (b)Working in the heat.
        • (4)Check your urine color to determine hydration.
        • (5)Urine that is the color of lemonade is good, apple juice is not.
      • d.Mindset - The 80/20 Rule
        • (1)The goal is to eat well 80% of the time and enjoy other foods outside these guidelines 20% of the time.
        • (2)This can equate to 150-300 calories each day or including other foods at three (3) or less meals per week.
        • (3)Enjoy your favorite foods, but eat less.
        • (4)Avoid oversize portions.
        • (5)Creating limits, helps enjoy a well-balanced diet and your favorite foods.
      • e.Recover
        • (1)Do not waste your workout.
          • (a)Eat within 30-60 minutes after exercise to maximize energy stores and start muscle recovery.
          • (b)Eating right away prevents extreme hunger and overeating later.
        • (2)Sleep
          • (a)Whenever you can, get 7-9 hours of sleep.
          • (b)Chronic lack of sleep increases stress hormones in the body, decreases reaction time, and does not allow the body to recover properly.
    • 4.Develop Healthy Eating Habits
      • a.Now is the time to develop healthy eating habits.
      • b.Pay attention to the regimen in training and practice making good choices.
      • c.Try new foods while you are here, such as:
        • (1)Adding different vegetables to a salad.
        • (2)Adding fruit to whole grain cereal.
      • d.You must continuously make an effort to ensure you fuel and hydrate your body well.
      • e.Once training is over, the easy choice to return to old habits or turn to other foods, such as:
        • (1)Energy drinks
        • (2)Frequent fast foods
        • (3)High fat and sugar snacks that do not promote optimal performance.
      • f.Remember that you can balance your favorite foods and still provide your body with what it needs.
      • g.There will always be choices.
      • h.Use these simple guidelines to make wise choices.
      • i.Resources:
        • (1)Every command has at least one Command Fitness Leader (CFL).
        • (2)CFLs have access to credible nutrition and fitness resources to assist in developing good habits.
        • (3)Seek assistance if you feel you need more guidance on how to improve your nutrition.
