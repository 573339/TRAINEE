C. Topic Outline:

  1. Introduction
  2. Guidelines on Changing Nutrition Habits
    1. The foods you eat play a large role in how you perform, and how you feel.
    2. Poor nutrition leads to:
      1. Low energy
      2. Injury and disease
      3. Could result in early release from the Navy if body fat standards are not continuously met.
    3. Simple steps are provided to help develop your own plan to achieve your performance goals.
    4. If you want to be successful, use your time training to establish good habits that you can continue.
  3. Navy Operational Fueling Services
    1. Developed to provide a world class nutrition tool to simplify nutrition guidelines.
    2. Scientifically based and a collaboration between Navy Dietitians and the Athletic Performance Institute (API).
    3. Terminology used in this program is exactly what they use when teaching nutrition to professional athletes from all sports.
    4. Navy teamed up with API to bring this tool to Sailors.
  4. Five Fundamental Principles That Assist in Developing a Successful Nutrition Plan
    1. Eat Clean
      1. Come back to Earth -- Choose the least processed foods.
        1. “Whole” foods such as:
          1. Fruits
          2. Vegetables
          3. Lean Protein (meat, beans, nuts)
          4. Low Fat Milk or Yogurt (non-fat or 1%)
          5. Whole grains that include the word “whole” as the first ingredient on the food label.
        2. Processed foods are those found at most fast food restaurants and in the freezer section of the grocery store.
        3. They do not resemble the food that came from the ground or the animal. Think of a hot dog.
      2. Eat Color - Eat a variety of fruits or vegetables daily.
        1. Naturally occurring color in food means more nutrients.
        2. Try to have at least colors at each meal, such as:
          1. Yellow
          2. Orange
          3. Green
          4. Red
          5. White
          6. Blue/Purple
        3. Make half your plate fruits, vegetables and grains.
        4. Artificial color does not count, such as:
          1. Candy
          2. Food Dye on Icing
      3. The less the better - Include lean sources of protein daily.
        1. Choose protein from sources without legs.
          1. Fish
          2. Shrimp
          3. Black Eyed Peas
          4. Kidney Beans
        2. Choose protein from sources with two legs.
          1. Chicken
          2. Turkey
          3. Egg
        3. Limit the ones with four legs.
          1. Burgers
          2. Bacon
          3. Pork Chops
          4. Hot Dogs
          5. Sausage
      4. Eat healthy fats.
        1. Healthy fats provide energy and keep you feeling full.
        2. Choose most of your fat from plant sources.
          1. Nuts
          2. Oils
          3. Seeds
        3. Include Omega 3 fatty acids
          1. Tuna
          2. Salmon
          3. Walnuts
          4. Flaxseeds
        4. Limit fat from animal sources
          1. Butter
          2. High fat meats (bacon)
          3. Fried foods
    2. Eat Often
      1. Eat breakfast every day.
      2. Make every attempt to eat something at breakfast every day.
      3. It does not have to be breakfast foods.
      4. Choose a lean protein source and some fruit, dairy and/or whole grains.
      5. Spread calories out over the day to maintain energy levels and prevent overeating at night.
    3. Hydrate
      1. Stay hydrated.
      2. Drink water instead of sugary beverages, such as:
        1. Soda
        2. Sweet Tea
      3. Fruit Beverages, Electrolyte replacement drinks are not needed unless:
        1. Exercising for greater than 90 minutes.
        2. Working in the heat.
      4. Check your urine color to determine hydration.
      5. Urine that is the color of lemonade is good, apple juice is not.
    4. Mindset - The 80/20 Rule
      1. The goal is to eat well 80% of the time and enjoy other foods outside these guidelines 20% of the time.
      2. This can equate to 150-300 calories each day or including other foods at 3 or less meals per week.
      3. Enjoy your favorite foods, but eat less.
      4. Avoid oversize portions.
      5. Creating limits helps enjoy a well-balanced diet and your favorite foods.
    5. Recover
      1. Don’t waste your workout.
        1. Eat within 30-60 minutes after exercise to maximize energy stores and start muscle recovery.
        2. Eating right away prevents extreme hunger and overeating later.
      2. Sleep
        1. Whenever you can, get 7-9 hours of sleep.
        2. Chronic lack of sleep increases stress hormones in the body, decreases reaction time and does not allow the body to recover properly.
  5. Develop Healthy Eating Habits
    1. Now is the time to develop healthy eating habits.
    2. Pay attention to the regiment in training and practice making good choices.
    3. Try new foods while you are here, such as:
      1. Adding different vegetables to a salad.
      2. Adding fruit to whole grain cereal.
    4. You must continuously make an effort to ensure you fuel and hydrate your body well.
    5. Once training is over, the easy choice will be to return to old habits or turn to other foods, such as:
      1. Energy drinks
      2. Frequent fast foods
      3. High fat and sugar snacks that do not promote optimal performance.
    6. Remember that you can balance your favorite foods and still provide your body with what it needs.
    7. There will always be choices.
    8. Use these simple guidelines to make wise choices.
    9. Resources:
      1. Every command has at least one command Fitness Leader (CFL).
      2. CFLs have access to credible nutrition and fitness resources to assist in developing good habits.
      3. There is a lot of misinformation available.
      4. Seek assistance if you feel you need more guidance on how to improve your nutrition.
