  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Definitions of Terms
      • a.Force - To do violence. Compel by physical or intellectual means, or to achieve or win by strength in struggle or violence.
      • b.Serious Bodily Harm - A fracture or dislocated bone, deep cut, torn members of the body, serious damage to the internal organs and other life-threatening injuries.
      • c.Deadly Force - Force that a person uses causing, or that a person knows or should know, would create a substantial risk of causing death or serious bodily harm.
    • 2.Deadly Force Guidance
      • a.Deadly force is justified only under conditions of extreme necessity, and when lesser means have been exhausted, are unavailable, or cannot be reasonably employed.
      • b.Scenarios where Deadly Force is authorized
        • (1)Self Defense and Defense of Other DoD Personnel
        • (2)Defense of Others
        • (3)Assets Vital to National Security
        • (4)Assets not involving national security but inherently dangerous to others.
        • (5)National Critical Infrastructure
        • (6)Arrest, Apprehension, or preventing escape
        • (7)Defending against animals
    • 3.Deadly Force Triangle
      • a.Opportunity - Being within range of inflicting serious bodily harm.
      • b.Capability - Having the ability to inflict serious bodily harm.
      • c.Intent - Demonstrates aggression or actions to inflict serious bodily harm.
    • 4.Clearing Barrel
      • a.The sole purpose of the Clearing Barrel is to provide a safe direction to point a weapon while performing any of the following functions:
        • (1)Load and Make Ready
        • (2)Unload
        • (3)Show Clear
      • b.Clearing Barrel procedures will be posted near the clearing barrel.
      • c.Clearing Barrel Supervisors are required to read each command while monitoring the process.
      • d.Prior to using a clearing barrel:
        • (1)Inspect the general condition of the clearing barrel.
        • (2)Inspect areas behind and adjacent to clearing barrel.
        • (3)Clear unnecessary personnel away from clearing barrel.
    • 5.Safety is the NUMBER 1 concern at RTC Live Fire.
      • a.Safety Concerns
      • b.We will inspect the weapon for you
      • c.Never leave a weapon unattended
      • d.No horseplay
      • e.No running in this building
      • f.Weapons and ammunition will not be handled if anyone is downrange
      • g.Proper Personal Protective Equipment is required while firing small arms.
      • h.Conducting a Weapons Safety Check
        • (1)When taking possession of a weapon, conduct a four point safety check:
          • (a)Safety - Ensure weapon safety is engaged.
          • (b)Magazine - Ensure magazine well is empty
          • (c)Chamber - Ensure chamber is clear
          • (d)Safety - Again, ensure weapon safety is engaged
    • 6.Universal Weapons Safety Rules
      • a.Proper weapons handling is the responsibility of the person in control of the weapon. When armed with any weapon for which chambering a round has been authorized, personnel must ensure that safety remains in the safe position until immediately prior to firing
      • b.There are FOUR rules that must be observed at all times, whether in training or in combat. These rules are the foundation responsible weapons handling
      • c.Observing these four safety rules will ensure safe and effective weapon handling both on and off the range.
        • (1)Rule 1: Treat every weapon as if it were loaded. This rule is intended to prevent unintentional injury to personnel or damage to property from handling or transferring possession of a weapon.
        • (2)Rule 2: Never point a weapon at anything you do not intend to shoot. This rule enforces the importance of muzzle awareness and reinforces positive identification of the target.
        • (3)Rule 3: Keep your finger straight and off the trigger until you are ready to fire. This rule is intended to minimize the risk of firing the weapon negligently
        • (4)Rule 4: Keep weapon on SAFE until you intend to fire. This rule enforces the use of the weapon's safety feature.
    • 7.Components of the M9 Pistol
      • a.Front and Rear Sights - Used to align weapon and obtain sight alignment / sight picture.
      • b.Slide Assembly - After pistol is fired, the recoil of the assembly:
        • (1)Ejects the empty cartridge.
        • (2)Cocks the hammer.
        • (3)Loads next round.
      • c.Decocking/Safety lever - Used to "safe" the weapon. When the safety is on, the:
        • (1)Hammer is released to the forward position.
        • (2)Weapon will NOT fire.
      • d.Slide Stop - After the last round is fired, automatically holds the slide to the rear.
      • e.Hammer - When trigger is squeezed, drops forward causing the round to fire.
      • f.Pistol Grip - Provides for proper placement of hands for firing.
        • (1)Proper Two Handed Grip - Back-strap fits into "V" created by thumb, fingers, and palm. Thumbs stacked on same side. Wrist locked to prevent vertical displacement of shots.
        • (2)Finger Position - Trigger finger along slide assembly until ready to fire.
      • g.Trigger - Used to fire weapon.
        • (1)Always squeeze trigger. Do not jerk trigger.
      • h.Magazine - max capacity is 15 rounds
      • i.Magazine Release Button - When depressed, releases magazine from pistol.
    • 8.Fundamentals of handling the M9 Pistol
      • a.Improper Two Handed Grip - Thumbs are NOT stacked on the same side. There is an increased chance of personnel injury when using Improper Two Handed Grip.
      • b.Proper Two Handed Grip - Back-strap fits into “V” created by thumb, fingers and palm Thumbs are stacked on the same side. Wrist is locked to prevent vertical displacement of shots.
      • c.Improper Sight Alignment - Front or rear sight are NOT used in conjunction with each other causing the shooter to miss intended point of impact.
      • d.Proper Sight Alignment - Front and rear sight should be level with equal amount of light on each side of the front sight when looking through the rear sight.
      • e.Finger Position - Always squeeze trigger. Do no jerk the trigger.
    • 9.Procedures for Loading and Unloading the Pistol
      • a.Pistol Loading
        • (1)Insert a loaded magazine into the magazine of the pistol until a click is heard. This ensures proper placement of the magazine.
        • (2)To load first round, press down on slide stop.
      • b.Pistol Unloading/Magazine Removal
        • (1)Keep your weapon level and pointed down range at your target.
        • (2)Place decocking/safety lever in the "SAFE" (down) position.
        • (3)If you are left-handed, ensure your right hand is under the magazine. Depress the magazine release button with your right middle finger.
        • (4)If you are right-handed, ensure your left hand is under the magazine. Depress the magazine release button with your right thumb.
    • 10.Pistol Stances
      • a.Standing Isosceles
        • (1)Face the target.
        • (2)Stand with body erect and balanced.
        • (3)Ensure your toes are pointed forward and feet are placed shoulder-width apart.
        • (4)Ensure the weapon is level, pointed down range and in the upright position.
        • (5)Keep your finger OFF the trigger.
      • b.Kneeling Isosceles
    • 11.Weapon Condition Codes
      • a.Prior to loading the pistol, the weapon handler should be knowledgeable of weapon-specific state and condition codes. These codes enable the weapons handler to quickly identify weapon status and clearly convey that information to others.
      • b.A weapon's readiness to fire and its corresponding safety status are described by one of four weapon condition codes.
      • c.The condition codes for the M9 Service Pistol are:
        • (1)Condition 4: Magazine removed, slide forward, NO round chambered, safety on.
        • (2)Condition 3: Magazine inserted, slide forward, NO round chambered, safety on.
        • (3)Condition 2: Not Applicable.
        • (4)Condition 1: Magazine inserted, slide forward, round chambered, safety on.
    • 12.Range Operations
      • a.Personal Protective Equipment:
        • (1)Small Arms Personal Protection Equipment (PPE)
          • (a)In addition to following and applying range safety procedures when handling and firing small arms, you should take steps to protect your hearing, particularly when you are exposed to repeated small arms fire, such as on a firing range.
          • (b)Hearing protection is required at all times when firearms are being discharged during training and qualification.
          • (c)Approved hearing protection such as sound attenuators that reduce noise levels to 84 decibels or below is required.
          • (d)There are two general types of ear protective devices:
            • 1)Ear Plugs (Insert Type)
            • 2)Circumorial (Mickey Mouse ears)
          • (e)Blast noise from small arms fire may cause a temporary or permanent hearing loss. The extent of injury depends on a number of factors, such as intensity of the noise, length of exposure, your own sensitivity to noise hazards and type of hearing protection utilized.
          • (f)When conducting range operations on an indoor range; both types of hearing protection are required due to increased intensity level of noise.
          • (g)Shatterproof eye protection is required when shooting. American National Standards Institute (ANSI) approved glasses are required.
          • (h)Once the range has been declared "Hot" (live fire exercises have commenced) all personnel on the range are required to have safety glasses and hearing protection. This rule applies to all personnel on the firing line and/or behind the ready line whom are observing.
      • b.Range Safety Officer (RSO):
        • (1)All firing sequences and range commands shall be given by the RSO or an instructor under the close supervision of the RSO. Shooters must listen carefully, concentrate on, and adhere to all instructions and commands given by the RSO.
      • c.Range Coaches (Line Coach):
        • (1)Responsible to the RSO - They have direct and immediate authority over the shooter(s) under their purview while on the firing line.
        • (2)Line coaches will be on the firing line behind the shooter(s), and will assist with shooting fundamentals.
        • (3)They will analyze, score targets, assist in performing remedial actions to clear stoppages/malfunctions, and ensure all safety rules are being followed.
      • d.Live Fire Range Commands
        • (1)Load and Make Ready: Insert Magazine, press slide stop.
        • (2)Holster: Double tap on the shoulder.
        • (3)Standby: Grip weapon, but DO NOT draw.
        • (4)Commence Fire: Shoot the course of fire. Non-verbal, target lights on.
        • (5)Cease Fire: Lights off, stop shooting. Line coach hand in front of face, stop shooting.
        • (6)Can also be called when there is an unsafe condition, which could cause death or serious bodily harm if operations are not halted immediately.
      • e.Course of fire
        • (1)3 yard line - 12 rounds
        • (2)7 yard line - 12 rounds
        • (3)15 yard line - 24 rounds
      • f.Scoring - This is a qualification course. Minimum score required 180. Course counts towards earning a ribbon/medal.
      • g.Weapons Malfunctions
        • (1)If you experience a weapons malfunction:
          • (a)Hangfire: Delay in the detonation of gunpowder
          • (b)Misfire: Failure of round to fire
        • (2)DO keep your weapon pointed downrange
        • (3)DO signal a TTO
        • (4)Your Line Coach will assist you once "Cease Fire" has been called
        • (5)DO NOT attempt to fix the problem yourself
    • 13.Safety Violations
      • a.Range safety violations for which a shooter SHALL be removed from the firing line:
        • (1)Shooting after “Cease Fire” (lights off)
        • (2)Shooting with an individual "Cease Fire" (hand in front of your face)
        • (3)Attempting to clear a malfunction (rack the slide)
        • (4)Bending down to pick up something
        • (5)Attempting to holster an UNSAFE weapon
        • (6)Crossing the RED firing line
        • (7)Failure to follow instructions (FFI)
        • (8)Negligent discharge
        • (9)Breaking blue
    • 14.Safety violations SHALL result in disciplinary actions
      • a.Negligent discharge resulting in death or serious bodily hard to anyone:
        • (1)Courts Martial = Going to Jail
      • b.Major safety violation or negligent discharge not resulting in harm:
        • (1)Possible set back in training
