• A.Introduction
    • This course is an look into the U.S. Navy enlisted to officer commission programs.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • EXPLAIN the Navy enlisted to officer commissioning programs.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.U.S. Naval Academy
      • a.Located in Annapolis, Maryland.
      • b.Students (Midshipmen) receive pay, tuition, and room and board.
      • c.Graduates:
        • (1)Receive Bachelor of Science degrees in areas such as engineering, mathematics, and physics.
        • (2)Are commissioned as Ensigns, U.S. Navy Reserve or Second Lieutenants, U.S. Marine Corps Reserve.
      • d.Upon commissioning as an officer, the minimum service obligation is five (5) years active duty and three years reserve.
      • e.Applicants must be:
        • (1)Citizens of the U.S.
        • (2)Of good moral character and have no record of disciplinary action within three years prior to application.
        • (3)From 17 to 23 years old.
        • (4)Unmarried, not pregnant, and have no legal responsibility to support children or others.
        • (5)Physically qualified.
        • (6)Officially nominated by the Secretary of the Navy or one of the many nomination sources available.
        • (7)Recommended by their Commanding Officer.
    • 2.Seaman to Admiral-21 (STA-21) Program
      • a.A full-time undergraduate education and follow-on commissioning program for enlisted personnel in the Navy or Navy Reserve.
      • b.Participants can earn a college degree and a commission as a Line Officer; Nurse Corps, Supply Corps or Civil Engineer Corps. Officer or Special Duty Officer in Cryptology or Intelligence.
      • c.Benefits include:
        • (1)Up to $10,000 per year to cover tuition, fees, and books.
        • (2)Full Navy pay, allowances and benefits.
        • (3)Participants remain advancement eligible.
      • d.STA-21 participants are NOT eligible for:
        • (1)Tuition assistance.
        • (2)Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB) benefits.
      • e.Applicants must be:
        • (1)Citizens of the U.S. This cannot be waived.
        • (2)Recommended by their Commanding Officer as having good moral character, officer potential and unquestionable loyalty to the U.S.
        • (3)Serving on active duty, including the Reserves
        • (4)High school graduate or GED holder.
        • (5)Able to complete requirements for a baccalaureate degree in 36 months and before 31st birthday.
        • (6)Physically qualified.
        • (7)Without disciplinary action for three years before application.
      • f.STA-21 has 2 components:
        • (1)Naval Science Institute (NSI)
          • (a)Located in Newport, Rhode Island.
          • (b)8-week intensive officer preparation and indoctrination program.
          • (c)Attended by all STA-21 participants.
        • (2)Naval Reserve Officer Training Corps (NROTC)
          • (a)Located in NROTC-affiliated universities.
          • (b)Full-time, year-round study for up to 36 months.
    • 3.Officer Candidate School (OCS)
      • a.Located at Officer Training Command, Newport, Rhode Island.
      • b.A twelve (12) week commissioning program.
      • c.Programs are offered for both line and staff corps officers.
      • d.Upon reporting to OCS, selectees who are E-4 or below are advanced to PO2 (E5) and personnel E-5 and above remain at their current pay grades until graduation.
      • e.Graduates are appointed as Ensigns, U.S. Navy Reserve.
      • f.There is a minimum active duty obligation of 4 years.
      • g.Applicants must be:
        • (1)U.S. Citizens. This cannot be waived.
        • (2)Of good moral character and have no record of disciplinary action within three years prior to application.
        • (3)At least nineteen (19) years old.
        • (4)College graduates.
        • (5)Physically qualified.
        • (6)Recommended by their Commanding Officer.
    • 4.Medical Enlisted Commissioning Program (MECP)
      • a.A program for enlisted personnel to attend college full-time and earn a bachelorâ€™s degree in nursing.
      • b.While in school, students receive full pay and allowances and are eligible for advancement; however, students pay for their tuition, fees, and books.
      • c.Graduates receive commissions as Ensigns in the Nurse Corps, U.S. Navy Reserve and will attend Officer Development School (ODS) located in Newport RI.
      • d.Following commissioning, there is an eight (8) year obligation, with a minimum of four years on active duty.
      • e.Applicants must:
        • (1)Be citizens of the U.S. This cannot be waived.
        • (2)Be of good moral character and have no record of disciplinary action within three years before application.
        • (3)Be serving on active duty in the U.S. Navy, or Navy Reserve.
        • (4)Be able to complete a nursing degree and be commissioned before their thirty five (35) birthday.
        • (5)Be high school graduates or GED holder.
        • (6)Be physically qualified.
        • (7)Be recommended by their Commanding Officer.
    • 5.Medical Service Corps In-service Procurement Program (MSC IPP)
      • a.Provides a pathway to officer commissions for active duty enlisted personnel who meet eligibility criteria for either:
        • (1)Health Care Administration
        • (2)Physician Assistant
        • (3)Radiation Health Officer
        • (4)Environmental Health Officer
        • (5)Industrial Health Officer
        • (6)Entomology
        • (7)Pharmacy
      • b.Enlisted service members who want to complete a qualifying graduate degree may go to college full-time for 24 to 48 months with benefits that include:
        • (1)Tuition
        • (2)Mandatory fees
        • (3)Book allowance
        • (4)Full pay and allowances for their pay grade
        • (5)Eligibility for advancement
      • c.Once the degree is earned, personnel are commissioned as lieutenant (junior grade) in the Medical Service Corps and will attend Officer Development School (ODS).
      • d.Upon Commissioning, selectees will incur a minimum 8-year military service obligation, 3 of which must be served on active duty.
      • e.Applicants must:
        • (1)Be citizens of the U.S. This cannot be waived.
        • (2)Not have reached their 35th birthday by the time of initial appointment.
        • (3)Be of good moral character and have no record of disciplinary action within three years before application.
        • (4)Be serving on active duty in the U.S. Navy, or Navy Reserve in pay grade E5 through E9.
        • (5)Possess a qualifying degree of have a minimum amount of college credit for their chosen specialty.
        • (6)Be physically qualified.
        • (7)Be recommended by their Commanding Officer.
    • 6.Limited Duty Officer (LDO)program
      • a.Provides commissioning opportunities for senior enlisted and Chief Warrant Officers who are applying for LTJG.
      • b.LDOs are technically oriented officers who:
        • (1)Perform duties limited to specific occupational fields.
        • (2)Have strong managerial skills.
      • c.Selectees must attend the LDO/CWO Academy located at Officer Training Command, Newport, RI.
      • d.Personnel selected for LDO receive an initial appointment as Ensigns.
      • e.There is a minimum active duty obligation of four years.
      • f.Applicants must:
        • (1)Be citizens of the U.S., this cannot be waived.
        • (2)Be of good moral character and have no record of disciplinary action within three years before application.
        • (3)Be serving on active duty in the U.S. Navy, or Navy Reserve in pay grades E-6 through E-8. Personnel that are E6 must be selection board eligible for E-7.
        • (4)Have at least eight years of service but not more than 16 years of active naval service.
        • (5)Be high school graduates or GED holders.
        • (6)Be physically qualified and meet physical fitness standards.
        • (7)Be recommended by their Commanding Officer.
    • 7.Chief Warrant Officer (CWO) Program
      • a.Provide commissioning opportunities for senior enlisted personnel.
      • b.CWOs are technical specialists who perform duties requiring extensive knowledge and skills of a specific occupational field.
      • c.Selecteeâ€™s must attend the LDO/CWO Academy located at Officer Training Command, Newport, RI.
      • d.Personnel selected for CWO receive an initial appointment as CWO2.
      • e.There is a minimum active duty obligation of four (4) years.
      • f.Applicants must:
        • (1)Be citizens of the U.S. This cannot be waived.
        • (2)Be of good moral character and have no record of disciplinary action within three years before application.
        • (3)Be serving on active duty in the U.S. Navy, or Navy Reserve in pay grades E-7 through E-9; this includes E-6 personnel that have been selected or authorized advancement to CPO.
        • (4)Have at least twelve by not more than twenty-four years of service.
        • (5)Be high school graduates or GED holders.
        • (6)Be physically qualified and meet physical fitness standards.
        • (7)Be recommended by their Commanding Officer.
