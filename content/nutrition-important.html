• 1.Energy Balance Is Key
      • a.Energy in = Food and fluids consumed
      • b.Energy out = Calories burned by the body
      • c.If you take in more calories than you burn, your body stores the extra as fat and you gain weight.
      • d.If you take in fewer calories than you use, your body burns stored calories and you lose weight.
      • e.When energy in equals energy out, body weight is stable.
    • 2.Energy In – Manage Weight Through Good Food Habits
      • a.Eat a variety of foods every day (Use the ChooseMyPlate.gov).
      • b.Vary the colors in your diet by eating at least five (5) servings of fruits & vegetables per day.
      • c.Eat three (3) meals a day to distribute calories for better energy utilization. Do not skip breakfast.
      • d.Eat smaller portions of foods.
      • e.Reduce high-sugar foods (sodas, candy, desserts, limit fruit juice).
      • f.Limit high fat foods (fried, mayonnaise or dairy based sauces or dressings).
      • g.Avoid a severe calorie restriction.
      • h.Keep a diet journal when attempting to lose weight.
    • 3.Energy In – Carbohydrates
      • a.The body uses carbohydrates for immediate energy.
      • b.Carbohydrates (starch/sugars) can comprise up to 50%-65% of calories (5-7 g per kg body weight).
      • c.Food sources include bread, cereal, pasta, rice, beans, fruit, and vegetables.
      • d.Choose whole grains including whole grain breads, legumes, dried beans, fruits, vegetables.
    • 4.Energy In – Protein
      • a.Necessary for rebuilding and repairing body tissue.
      • b.Approximately 15% of calories should come from protein (0.8-1.4 g per kg body weight).
      • c.Food sources are meat, fish, poultry, eggs, beans, milk.
      • d.Choose lean sources of protein (non-fried, without skin).
      • e.Protein is used for energy if fat or carbohydrate is lacking in the diet.
      • f.Choose seafood at least twice a week as a main protein food.
    • 5.Energy In – Fat
      • a.Fat is needed by the body for organ and nerve tissue.
      • b.Fat in the diet carries the vitamins A, D, E, and K to be stored by the liver.
      • c.Fat should be about 25-35% of calories, but only 7% of the calories should come from saturated fat or animal fat (butter, bacon, fried food, baked goods).
      • d.Fat is calorie dense (9 calories/gram), so portions should be small.
      • e.Limit animal sources including cheese, sausages, hot dogs, bacon, and butter.
      • f.Choose healthy plant fats including olive or canola oil nuts and seeds.
      • g.Look for seafood rich in omega-3 fatty acids, such as salmon, trout, tuna, and herring.
      • h.Do not strive for a fat free diet. Fat is essential. Moderation is the key.
    • 6.Energy In – Calcium
      • a.Main source is dairy products (milk, yogurt, cheese and calcium fortified soymilk). Choose low fat (1% or less) choices.
      • b.You need at least three servings daily (1000+ mg).
      • c.Calcium is good for maintaining strong bones and teeth. If you do not get enough, bones can weaken – leading to stress fractures, broken bones, etc.
    • 7.Energy In – Water
      • a.Needed by every cell of the body.
      • b.Drink at least 8 cups of water daily.
      • c.High protein/high sodium diets increase water needs.
      • d.Drink more when increasing fiber intake to prevent constipation.
      • e.Increase fluid intake when exercising a lot to replace sweat losses.
      • f.All beverages except for alcohol, count – choose low calorie for better weight control.
    • 8.Energy In – How Do Vitamins Fit In?
      • a.Vitamins and minerals do not provide energy – they can help our bodies get energy from the foods we eat.
      • b.Needed to regulate body systems, build/repair tissue, prevent diseases, etc.
      • c.Supplements are not warranted if you are eating a balanced diet with plenty of variety.
      • d.Do your own research before choosing to take any supplement.
    • 9.Energy Out – Exercise Advantage
      • a.Engage in aerobic exercise 3 – 6 times/week (walk, jog, cycle, etc.).
      • b.Engage in strength training exercise 2 – 3 times/week.
      • c.Increase lifestyle physical activity (taking stairs, parking far away).
    • 10.Energy Out – Exercise (FITT Rule)
      • a.Frequency: Almost every day
      • b.Intensity: The higher the intensity, the more calories you burn in a given time period. Work hard enough to get your heart rate up but still be able to carry on a little conversation.
      • c.Time: 30 to 60 minutes
      • d.Type: Aerobic and strength
    • 11.Energy Out – Plan Ahead
      • a.Plan your daily schedule so that exercise is a regular part of the routine.
      • b.Exercise burns calories and helps to manage stress.
