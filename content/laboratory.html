Outline Sheet 1-17-1

A. Introduction

In the firefighting laboratory, you will apply the knowledge learned in your firefighting classes to extinguish fires. During your Navy career, you will most likely be in situations where you will have to fight fires; your life may depend on your skills as a firefighter.

B. Enabling Objective(s):

  • 14.1 EGRESS from a dark, smoke and heat-filled compartment as a member of a small group.
  • 15.23 EXPLAIN the causes and procedures to control a wild hose.
  • 15.24 EXTINUISH a class “A: fire using fire hoses and vari-nozzles connected to the firemain system as a member of a hose team, while wearing an oxygen breathing apparatus and firefighting protective gear.
  • 15.25 EXTINUISH a class “B” fire using fire hoses and vari-nozzles connected to the aqueous film forming foam (AFFF) system (discharging water), as a member of a hose team, while wearing an oxygen breathing apparatus and firefighting protective gear.
  • 15.26 EXTINUISH a class “C” fire using a portable CO2 fire extinguisher, while wearing a self-contained breathing apparatus and firefighting protective gear.

C. Topic Outline:

  1. Introduction

  3. Eductor Demonstration
    1. Inline eductor with P-100 pump for firefighting.
    2. Peri-jet eductor with P-100 for dewatering.
  4. Gaining Control of a Wild Hose
    1. Causes
      1. Hose team loses control of hose.
      2. Unmanned hose inadvertently charged.
    2. Procedures
      1. Secure wye-gate.
      2. Secure water flow at the fireplug.
      3. Make a Z-kink at a nonmoving point on the hose 1 ½-inch hose only).
      4. Advance along the hose from a nonmoving point toward the nozzle.
