• A.Introduction
    • Early in your Navy career, most learning will be in a classroom to include recruit training and “A” school. Success in these learning environments will depend on the use of good listening and note-taking skills.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • EXPLAIN active listening.
    EXPLAIN how an active listener prepares to listen.
    IDENTIFY distractions that may inhibit learning and how to overcome them.
    IDENTIFY the purpose of note taking during class.
    EXPLAIN how to prepare for note taking.
    IDENTIFY helpful note-taking tips.
    EXPLAIN how to use notes for study.
    EXPLAIN the five steps for taking and using notes.
    IDENTIFY the purpose of tests.
    EXPLAIN how to prepare for a test.
    IDENTIFY the types of questions used on Recruit Training tests.

    EXPLAIN the relationship between the Navy Core Values and learning skills.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Active listening
      • a.Goes beyond hearing; it involves thinking and interacting with the information.
      • b.To ensure understanding, active listeners:
        • (1)Take notes.
        • (2)Ask questions.
        • (3)Separate the instructor’s opinion from fact.
      • c.Active listening will help you:
        • (1)Have a better understanding of the information.
        • (2)Retain information.
        • (3)Pass tests.
    • 2.How Active Listeners Prepare For Class
      • a.Read the assignment before class. Write down questions to ask your instructor.
      • b.Active listeners must have a positive attitude. Negative attitudes interfere with learning.
    • 3.Distractions That Can Inhibit Learning
      • a.Many classrooms have distractions, such as:
        • (1)Climate
        • (2)Background noises
        • (3)Poor acoustics
      • b.Watching the clock waiting for the class to end is also a distraction.
      • c.Try to maintain your concentration in spite of the problems.
      • d.If you are tired/sleepy, make sure you:
        • (1)Sit up straight in your desk or stand up, and request shipmates to help you stay awake.
        • (2)Take notes.
        • (3)Participate in the class; for example, ask questions.
        • (4)If easily distracted, sit at the front of the classroom.
    • 4.Purpose of Taking Notes During Class
      • a.Provides you with a record of important facts and information.
      • b.Reinforces learning.
      • c.Note-taking helps you to:
        • (1)Pay attention.
        • (2)Remember information.
        • (3)Organize key points.
        • (4)Study for tests.
    • 5.Prepare For Note-Taking
      • a.Be mentally and physically prepared to learn. Have a positive attitude.
      • b.To become familiar with the lesson, be committed to reading the:
        • (1)Enabling Objectives listed on the Outline Sheets in the Trainee Guide. The objectives designate the information that will be covered in class.
        • (2)Pages from The Bluejacket’s Manual indicated by the Assignment Sheet in your Trainee Guide.
      • c.Bring a pen and Trainee Guide to class.
    • 6.Note-Taking Tips
      • a.Listen carefully. Listening is the key to good note-taking.
      • b.Be aware of instructor signals that key important information, such as:
        • (1)Change in pitch of voice
        • (2)Gestures
        • (3)Facial expressions
      • c.Write quickly so you can get back to listening.
        • (1)Use abbreviations; for example, write “CO” instead of Commanding Officer.
        • (2)Do not worry about punctuation or spelling. They can be corrected later.
        • (3)Leave out non-essential words.
      • d.If you have questions, write them in your notes so you can ask your instructor or RDC later.
      • e.Ask the instructor to repeat information that you missed.
      • f.Always take accurate notes. Often someone from your division will be standing watch or be at an appointment and miss class. Take good notes so your shipmates can copy them.
    • 7.Using Notes For Study
      • a.Revise and edit notes as soon as possible after class. You may have difficulty in interpreting your notes if you wait too long.
        • (1)Fill in information that you may have missed while it is fresh in your mind.
        • (2)Write out words that you abbreviated and may not remember later.
        • (3)Correct spelling.
      • b.Go through your notes on the Outline and Assignment Sheets again and study them. Mark areas where you have questions to ask at night study.
    • 8.Five Steps for Taking and Using Notes
      • a.RECORD -- Write down main ideas and facts.
      • b.REDUCE -- Summarize what the instructor says; do NOT write everything word-for-word.
      • c.RECITE -- Re-enforce learning by explaining the content of your notes to someone else.
      • d.REFLECT -- Think about what you have written.
      • e.REVIEW -- Go over notes often, especially before a test.
    • 9.Purpose of Tests
      • a.Evaluate how well students are learning and performing.
      • b.Identify problem areas for each student.
      • c.Create a record of students’ progress.
      • d.Motivate students to do well.
    • 10.Preparation for the Test
      • a.Study training materials.
        • (1)Read the Enabling Objectives listed on the Outline Sheet in the Trainee Guide. All test questions and performance requirements are based on the Enabling Objectives.
        • (2)Review all Outline, diagram, and Information Sheets in the Trainee Guide.
        • (3)Read the pages from The Bluejacket’s Manual as indicated on the Assignment Sheets.
        • (4)Answer the study questions given on the Assignment Sheets.
      • b.Anticipate test questions. While studying, make a list of test questions that you think may be asked.
      • c.Avoid cramming, study a little bit each day.
      • d.Approach the test with confidence. Usually, the more you study, the more confident you’ll be.
    • 11.Types of Test Questions
      • a.Multiple choice
        • (1)Have two parts
        • (2)Stem -- States the problem.
          • (a)Alternatives
            • 1)The possible answers.
            • 2)Usually four (4).
            • 3)Only one (1) is correct.
        • (3)Read questions cautiously / carefully watch for key words and phrases.
          • (a)Qualifying words and statements
            • 1)Some qualifying words and statements limit or restrict.
              • a)Examples: Most, some, always.
              • b)Allow flexibility.
            • 2)Other qualifying words and statements are absolutes.
              • a)Examples: Always, never, all, none.
              • b)They restrict the meaning of the statement.
              • c)Are usually not the correct answer.
          • (b)Stems containing the words, “EXCEPT” or “NOT” must be answered by eliminating alternatives.
      • b.TRUE/FALSE
        • (1)Have two parts.
          • (a)Stem -- States the problem.
          • (b)Alternatives -- Two possible answers: true and false.
        • (2)Stems containing words such as “all”, “every”, “generally”, and “probably”, should be considered before answering.
          • (a)Alternatives with “all” and “every” tend to be false.
          • (b)Alternatives with “generally” and “probably” tend to be true.
        • (3)If any part of the stem is false, the answer is false.
        • (4)The stem must be entirely true to be marked true.
    • 12.Taking the Test
      • a.Listen to and follow directions of test proctor (examiner). When necessary, ask for clarification of directions.
      • b.Quickly preview test by answering the following questions:
        • (1)How many pages and questions are there?
        • (2)What types of questions are asked?
          • (a)Multiple choice
          • (b)TRUE/FALSE
        • (3)What is the time limit?
      • c.Answer the questions
        • (1)Read each question carefully.
        • (2)Answer all the questions you know.
          • (a)Read all the alternatives.
          • (b)Eliminate the alternatives that you know are wrong.
          • (c)Select an answer.
          • (d)Do NOT change an answer unless you are sure your second choice is correct. Frequently, your first choice is the correct one.
        • (3)Later, return to the questions you do not know; sometimes you can use information from one question to answer another.
        • (4)If using an answer sheet, pencil marks should be dark and fill the circle completely.
          • (a)Do NOT make stray marks on the answer sheet; they could result in improper scoring of your test.
          • (b)Periodically, make sure that the numbers on the test match those on the answer sheet.
      • d.Check your answers for the following, before submitting for scoring.
        • (1)Ensure you marked the answers intended.
        • (2)Check that only one answer is marked for each question.
        • (3)Be sure every question has been answered.
          • (a)Do NOT leave blank spaces.
          • (b)Guess if necessary.
    • 13.Relationship Between the Navy Core Values and Learning Skills
      • a.Honor
        • (1)It is DISHONORABLE to cheat on an examination.
        • (2)Do NOT cheat!
        • (3)Anyone caught cheating will receive an failure on the exam
        • (4)Cheaters are sent to their ship’s officer for disciplinary action.
      • b.Courage
        • (1)Have the courage to ask questions in class.
      • c.Commitment
        • (1)Actively listen to the instructor.
        • (2)Be committed to preparing for examinations by:
          • (a)Studying the reading assignments listed by Assignment in the Trainee Guide.
          • (b)Answering the study questions provided on Assignment.
          • (c)Taking notes and paying attention in class.
          • (d)Performing the best you can in laboratories, such as firefighting and line handling.
          • (e)Effectively using study time.
