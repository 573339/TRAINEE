A. Introduction

Early in your Navy career, most learning will be in a classroom; i.e., recruit training and “A” school; your success will depend on the use of good listening and note-taking skills.

As a person new to the fleet, you will have to know basic deck terminology in order to communicate with others. Basic knowledge about the ship is a requirement for successful performance of your job. The information in this lesson will help you understand the new surroundings on your first ship.

The following Naval Standards will be touched on in this lesson: P451, Q657. NAVPERS 18068F, Naval Standards for E1 through E3 may be found in the Recruit Trainee Guide

  12. Types of Test Questions
    1. Multiple choice
      1. Have two parts
        1. Stem -- States the problem.
        2. Alternatives
          1. The possible answers.
          2. Usually four (4).
          3. Only one (1) is correct.
      2. Read questions , watch for key words and phrases.
        1. Qualifying words and statements
          1. Some qualifying words and statements limit or restrict.
            1. Examples: Most, some, always.
            2. Allow flexibility.
          2. Other qualifying words and statements are absolutes.
            1. Examples: Always, never, all, none.
            2. They restrict the meaning of the statement.
            3. Are usually not the correct answer.
        2. Stems containing the words, “EXCEPT” or “NOT” must be answered by eliminating .
    2. TRUE/FALSE
      1. Have two parts.
        1. Stem -- States the problem.
        2. Alternatives -- Two possible answers: true and false.
      2. Stems containing words such as “all”, “every”, “generally”, and “probably”, should be considered before answering.
        1. Alternatives with “all” and “every” tend to be false.
        2. Alternatives with “generally” and “probably” tend to be true.
      3. If any part of the stem is false, the answer is false.
      4. The stem must be entirely true to be marked true.
  13. Taking the Test
    1. Listen to and follow directions of test proctor (examiner). When necessary, ask for of directions.
    2. Quickly preview test by answering the following questions:
      1. How many pages and questions are there?
      2. What types of questions are asked?
        1. Multiple choice
        2. TRUE/FALSE
      3. What is the time limit?
    3. Answer the questions
      1. Read each question .
      2. Answer all the questions you know.
        1. Read all the alternatives.
        2. Eliminate the alternatives that you know are wrong.
        3. Select an answer.
        4. Do NOT change an answer unless you’re sure your second choice is correct. Frequently, your choice is the correct one.
      3. Later, to the questions you don’t know; sometimes you can use information from one question to answer another.
      4. If using an answer sheet, pencil marks should be dark and fill the circle completely.
        1. Do NOT make stray marks on the answer sheet; they could result in improper scoring of your test.
        2. Periodically, make sure that the numbers on the test match those on the answer sheet.
    4. Check your answers for the following, before submitting for scoring.
      1. Ensure you marked the answers intended.
      2. Check that only one answer is marked for each question.
      3. Be sure has been answered.
        1. Do NOT leave blank spaces.
        2. Guess if necessary.
