A. Introduction

Drug and alcohol abuse can ruin your Navy career and your life, and result in your death. This lesson provides information about the Navy and drug and alcohol policy and related terminology.

B. Enabling Objective(s):

  • 19.1 IDENTIFY the purpose of the drug and alcohol instruction (OPNAVINST 5350.4).
  • 19.2 IDENTIFY the definitions of terms associated with drug and alcohol abuse.
  • 19.3 IDENTIFY the characteristics, reactions, and definitions of terms associated with designer drugs.
  • 19.4 IDENTIFY the major provisions of the U.S. Navy drug and alcohol policy.
  • 19.5 IDENTIFY individual responsibility and accountability.
  • 19.6 IDENTIFY the Navy’s responsible drinking campaign, "Keep What You’ve Earned".
  • 20.1 IDENTIFY the major provisions of the U.S. Navy tobacco use policy.

  3. Definitions of Terms Associated with Drug and Alcohol Abuse
    1. Alcohol related
      1. Abuse - The excessive or repeated use of alcohol that adversely affects the user’s health, family, or ability to perform on the job.
      2. Dependence -- Physical or psychological reliance on alcohol.
      3. Incident -- Behavior that is:
        1. Caused by the use of alcohol.
        2. Punishable under the UCMJ or civilian authority.
    2. Drug related
      1. Controlled substances -- Drugs or other substances that have their use and possession restricted by regulations. Controlled substances include:
        1. Cannabis -- Marijuana, THC, Hashish
        2. Narcotics -- Heroin, Morphine, Codeine, Methadone, Darvon
        3. Hallucinogens/psychedelics -- LSD, STP, PCP, Peyote, Psilocybin (magic mushroom)
        4. Depressants -- Tranquilizers, Methaqualone, Quaaludes, Phenobarbital, Valium, Librium
        5. Stimulants -- Cocaine, Amphetamines, Methamphetamines, Crystal Meth (ice)
        6. Anabolic steroids -- Stanozolol
        7. Designer (club) drugs -- Ecstasy (X, MDMA, Adam), Ketimine (Special K), GHB (G)
          1. Spice (K2), Methadrone (Bath Salts), Herbal incense (K3)
          2. Commonly found at:
            1. Dances or parties; usually non-alcoholic.
            2. Last all night.
            3. Target year-olds.
          3. Ecstasy is:
            1. Illegal.
            2. An amphetamine-based drug with hallucinogenic properties.
            3. Normally sold in tablet from (size and color vary) for $10-$30 per “hit” (dose).
            4. Growing in popularity.
            5. Tested for during Navy urinalysis screening.
          4. Designer drugs cause a different reaction in every person; no one can predict the outcome. Some reactions include:
            1. Hallucinations
            2. Paranoia
            3. Amnesia
          5. When used with alcohol, the effects of these and other drugs are even more harmful.
      2. Legal drugs
        1. Over the counter and prescribed medication taken as directed.
        2. A legal drug not taken as directed is considered drug abuse.
  4. U.S. Navy Drug and Alcohol Policy
    1. The Navy’s policy on:
      1. Drug abuse is " ".
      2. Alcohol is “responsible use”. Use of alcohol must NOT:
        1. Interfere with one’s military duty.
        2. Reduce one’s dependability.
        3. Reflect discredit on one’s self or the Navy.
    2. Alcohol abusers/alcoholics
      1. The Navy will provide rehabilitation for alcohol abusers that show for further useful service.
      2. Repeat offenders and personnel not motivated to reject their abuse are separated from the Navy.
    3. Drug abusers
      1. Will be separated from the military.
      2. All drug dependent service members will be offered treatment as part of discharge.
  5. Individual Responsibility and Accountability in Accordance with the Navy Drug and Alcohol Policy
    1. Responsibility
      1. All sailors are responsible for themselves and their shipmates to avoid:
        1. Excessive use of alcohol.
        2. The use of controlled substances. When with a shipmate, try to discourage excessive drinking.
    2. .
      1. Drug and alcohol abuse is punishable under the UCMJ.
      2. Disciplinary actions for drug/alcohol abuse include:
        1. Commanding Officer Non-judicial Punishment (Captain’s Mast).
        2. Trial by court-martial.
        3. Separation from the Navy.
      3. Discharges due to:
        1. Alcohol abuse are under "Other than Honorable".
        2. Drug abuse are "".
      4. Discharges because of drug or alcohol abuse have a negative impact on the offender’s future.
        1. The discharge remains a permanent part of a member’s Enlisted Service Record.
        2. May cause the service member to be:
          1. Refused employment.
          2. Denied veteran’s benefits.
      5. An arrest and/or conviction by civilian authorities does not exclude the possibility of prosecution by the Navy.
      6. In 2009 NATTC Pensacola FL, disciplined 28 Sailors under the UCMJ just for the use of Spice. This included loss of pay, extra duty, restriction, and reduction in rate.
  6. Policies of the Navy “Keep What You’ve Earned” Campaign
    1. Goals:
      1. Focus on achievements that could be lost due to poor choices regarding alcohol.
      2. Inform Sailors on what it means to drink responsibly.
      3. Promote and encourage alternatives to drinking.
      4. Enable leadership to assist with Sailor education.
      5. Create partnerships with civilian programs.
    2. - Not a Reputation.
      1. Only of U.S. 17-24 year olds are eligible for Navy service.
      2. Just one bad decision while drinking alcohol can result in:
        1. Loss of rank, rate or pay.
        2. Separation from the Navy.
        3. Civilian such a fines and/or jail time.
    3. Signs you may be drinking too much:
      1. No control over the amount you consume.
      2. Drinking is affecting your work or relationships.
      3. Poor judgment and risk taking.
      4. Difficulty remembering or .
      5. Angry and/or depressed moods.
  7. U.S. Navy Tobacco Use Policy
    1. Background
      1. Smoking causes cancer, heart disease, stroke, and lung disease.
      2. Tobacco products are cigarettes, pipe tobacco, cigars, or tobacco (chewing tobacco, snuff, dip, etc.).
      3. Tobacco use has been determined to be the most preventable cause of illness and death in the United States.
      4. World-wide over 6 million die annually due to tobacco related illnesses.
      5. 88% of adult smokers said they started at age 18.
      6. Cost in dollars, if you smoke a pack of cigarettes a day:
        1. At $7.00 a pack:
          1. $49 per week.
          2. $210 per month
          3. $2,555 per year.
    2. Policy
      1. It is the Navy’s policy to:
        1. Support abstinence.
        2. Discourage the use of tobacco products.
        3. Create a healthy working environment.
        4. Provide encouragement and assistance to stop smoking.
        5. Provide a tobacco cessation program.
      2. It is DON (Department of the Navy) policy that smoke-free DON facilities be established to protect all DON civilians and military personnel and members of the public visiting or using DON facilities from the health hazards caused by exposure to tobacco smoke.
      3. Recruits are prohibited from smoking while at or assigned to RTC including during liberty.
  8. Relationship between the Navy Core Values and the Navy’s Policies on Drug, Alcohol, and Tobacco Use
    1. Honor - We are honor bound to drink responsibly and abstain from using illegal drugs.
    2. Courage - Have the courage to get help if you have a problem with drugs or alcohol.
    3. Commitment - Be committed to upholding Navy policy on drug, alcohol, and tobacco use.
