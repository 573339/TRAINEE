In your Navy career, you will be exposed to many sources of stress. Learning how to recognize and deal with stress will have a lasting impact.

    1. Definitions
      1. Dictionary - A mental, emotional, or physical strain or tension.
      2. Other - Stress is any stressful event you may encounter or how you respond physically to a stressor.
      3. Navy’s definition: The “Process by which we respond to challenges to the body or mind”.
    2. Some specific types of stress you may encounter:
      1. Operational Stress.
    3. Stress is something everyone experiences daily and is:
      1. A normal part of life.
      2. Any demand on the mind or body.
      3. A natural reaction to everyday challenges.
    4. Operational stress is a double-edged sword; it presents both a danger and, if the stress is recognized and handled properly, an opportunity for growth. If can promote growth and push you to do your best. However, if there is too much stress, for too long, it can wear you down.
    5. Leaders and Shipmates can help you adapt to stress by providing:
      1. Tough realistic training.
      2. Knowledge, skills and attitudes necessary to help build resilience to stress related problems.

  5. Methods or Tools to Help Deal with Stress:
    1. - There are a couple of simple techniques that you can use to lower your stress level. They aren't complicated and you can do them almost anywhere when you feel stressed.
      1. - Breathing deeply interrupts your stress response and helps you to relax.
      2. Deep - Your body responds to tense thoughts or situations with muscle tension, which can cause pain or discomfort. Deep muscle relaxation reduces the muscle tension as well as general mental anxiety. Typically, it involves tensing individual muscle groups for several seconds and releasing the tension, allowing the muscles too gradually relax. Deep muscle relaxation is effective in combating stress related health problems and often helps people get to sleep.
