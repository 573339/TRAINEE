• A.Introduction
    • In your Navy career, you will be exposed to many sources of stress. Learning how to recognize and deal with stress will have a lasting impact.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • DEFINE stress.
    DEFINE the Stress Continuum Model.
    RECOGNIZE stress indicators in self and family.
    DESCRIBE methods (tools) to help deal with stress.
    IDENTIFY resources available for dealing with stress.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.What is Stress
      • a.Definitions
        • (1)Dictionary - A mental, emotional, or physical strain or tension.
        • (2)Other - Stress is any stressful event you may encounter or how you respond physically to a stressor.
        • (3)Navy’s definition: The “Process by which we respond to challenges to the body or mind”
      • b.Operational Stress
        • (1)Stress is something everyone experiences daily and is:
        • (2)A normal part of life.
        • (3)Any demand on the mind or body.
        • (4)A natural reaction to everyday challenges.
      • c.Operational stress is a double-edged sword; it presents both a danger and, if the stress is recognized and handled properly, an opportunity for growth. It can promote growth and push you to do your best. However, if there is too much stress, for too long, it can wear you down.
      • d.Leaders and shipmates can help you adapt to stress by providing:
        • (1)Tough, realistic training
        • (2)Knowledge, skills and attitudes necessary to help build resilience to stress-related problems.
    • 2.The Stress Continuum Model:
      • a.Developed by the USMC/Navy Medicine and Combat Operational Stress Control team. The goals of Operational Stress Control are to build resilience, get help for Sailors and families when needed, and to facilitate a way back to the “Ready” Green Zone.
      • b.The Continuum model has four color-coded zones:
        • (1)Ready (Green Zone)
        • (2)Reacting (Yellow Zone)
        • (3)Injured (Orange Zone)
        • (4)Ill (Red Zone)
      • c.Ready (Green Zone)
        • (1)Personnel who are not necessarily stress free but who remain resilient and functional.
      • d.Healthy: The Ready / Green Zone:
        • (1)Individuals in the “Ready” or Green Zone.
          • (a)Are calm and steady.
          • (b)Are confident and competent.
          • (c)Are successfully accomplishing their job.
          • (d)Have a good sense of humor.
          • (e)Get adequate rest.
          • (f)Eat a well-balanced diet.
          • (g)Are physically, socially and spiritually active.
      • e.Healthy (Ready) Commands in the Green Zone.
        • (1)Are highly trained and qualified.
        • (2)Maintain a positive command climate.
        • (3)Keep morale high.
        • (4)Promote strong unit cohesion.
        • (5)Maintain equipment properly.
        • (6)Maintain good order and discipline.
        • (7)Deglamorize alcohol and offer alternatives at command functions.
        • (8)Pay close attention to detail.
        • (9)Provide members with a clear sense of mission.
      • f.What happens at home can affect you in the workplace. Ready Green Zone families are those in which:
        • (1)Family members are actively involved with their siblings, parents, and children.
        • (2)Family members feel secure.
        • (3)Good communication exists between all family members.
        • (4)Home is clean and organized.
        • (5)Family members are engaged in family routines and activities.
        • (6)Siblings and children are functioning well at home and in school.
        • (7)Families are communicating well.
        • (8)Families are communicating and resolving conflicts effectively.
    • 3.Recognizing Stress Indicators In Self And Family:
      • a.Recognizing Stress: The Yellow Reacting Zone:
        • (1)Individuals in the Yellow Zone may:
          • (a)Feel anxious, irritable, or worried.
          • (b)Cut corners on the job or at home.
          • (c)Be grouchy or short tempered.
          • (d)Have difficulty sleeping.
          • (e)Have changes in eating patterns.
          • (f)Be apathetic or have loss of interest.
          • (g)Isolate themselves.
          • (h)Have a negative or pessimistic attitude.
        • (2)Families
          • (a)Families can also display signs of stress reactions. It is important to remember that many of these are normal reactions to challenging situations, but they may require some action from you and/or your leaders to help a return to the Green Zone. Signs of Reacting Families may include:
            • 1)Families arguing, not communicating effectively.
            • 2)Siblings and/or children acting out.
            • 3)Siblings and/or children having trouble in school.
            • 4)Family time compromised.
            • 5)Dropped Routines.
            • 6)Increased clutter and disorder.
            • 7)Increased financial difficulties.
            • 8)Reduced intimacy.
        • (3)What to do: Successfully handling a stressful event builds resilience and helps move you back to the Green Zone. Some things you can do to assist in handling a stressful event are:
          • (a)Get more rest.
          • (b)Work out regularly, eat right and stay hydrated.
          • (c)Spend time with people you trust.
          • (d)Attend to your spiritual needs.
          • (e)Redirect your attention away from the event.
          • (f)Encourage yourself and others.
          • (g)Practice breathing and muscle relaxation techniques.
          • (h)In addition to your actions, your leaders can assist in mitigating stress by doing positive after-action reviews and recognizing and rewarding accomplishments.
        • (4)Red Zone
          • (a)Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, Anxiety and Panic Disorders, and severe depression fall into the Red or Ill Zone. Symptoms you may experience include:
            • 1)Serve uncontrollable rage.
            • 2)Persistent uncontrollable shaking.
            • 3)Frequent panic
            • 4)Loss of emotional control or hysteria.
            • 5)Temporary blindness or deafness.
          • (b)Many injuries and illnesses exist in the Red Zone and include:
            • 1)Stress Impact.
            • 2)Traumatic Brain Injuries or TBI.
            • 3)Infections.
            • 4)Severe dehydration
          • (c)Seek medical evaluation to determine appropriate treatment when Red Zone indicators are present.
          • (d)These are usually NOT career-ending when treated early by appropriate medical personnel.
          • (e)Statistics show that most Sailors treated for these disorders return to full duty within a few months.
      • b.Goal of the OSC Program
        • (1)Is to keep Sailors in/or moving back toward the Green or Ready zone.
        • (2)Physically and psychologically healthy Sailors are better able to carry out their assigned responsibilities and duties in support of the Navy's mission.
        • (3)The bottom of the model reflects relative responsibilities of leaders, Sailors, families and caregivers.
        • (4)Recall that moving toward Green is a shared responsibility between you, your peers, your command leaders, your family, and supporting caregivers.
    • 4.Methods or Tools to Help Deal with Stress:
      • a.Relaxation Techniques - There are a couple of simple techniques that you can use to lower your stress level. They are not complicated and you can do them almost anywhere when you feel stressed.
        • (1)Deep Breathing - Breathing deeply interrupts your stress response and helps you to relax.
      • b.Deep muscle relaxation - Your body responds to tense thoughts or situations with muscle tension, which can cause pain or discomfort. Deep muscle relaxation reduces the muscle tension as well as general mental anxiety. Typically, it involves tensing individual muscle groups for several seconds and releasing the tension, allowing the muscles too gradually relax. Deep muscle relaxation is effective in combating stress related health problems and often helps people get to sleep.

      • f.Keep a journal: write notes to remind you of what you need to do and when.
      • g.Play a musical instrument.
