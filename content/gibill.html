  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Purpose of the Post 9/11 GI Bill
      • a.Provides financial assistance from Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to active duty military personnel and veterans to pay for education and training:
            • 1)Colleges and universities
            • 2)Business, technical and vocational schools
            • 3)Apprentice and on-the-job training programs
            • 4)Correspondence courses
            • 5)Flight training
            • 6)Remedial, deficiency, or refresher training
            • 7)License or certification tests
            • 8)High Tech Training
      • b.Provides entitlement for up to 36 months full-time school enrollment. (Example 9 months school with 3 months break would allow 4 years to complete your education.)
    • 2.MGIB Entitlement
      • a.To receive MGIB funds, members must complete an honorable period of active duty service.
      • b.Members receiving the Loan Repayment Program.
      • c.The following personnel are INELIGIBLE for MGIB enrollment:
            • 1)Members with prior active duty service
            • 2)Naval reservists on active duty time only for training.
            • 3)U.S. Naval Academy and other service academy graduates.
            • 4)Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC) scholarship graduates who received scholarship of more than $3,400 per school year.
      • d.Reservists enlisting under the National Call to Service (NCS) Program are not eligible for MGIB Program enrollment during Recruit Training. They may become eligible if permitted to extend or reenlist on active duty for at least 24 months after obligated NCS service.
    • 3.MGIB increased benefit option designed to increase monthly benefits under the MGIB if you qualify. Some service members may contribute up to an additional $600 to the GI Bill to receive increased monthly benefits.
    • 4.Under the Montgomery GI Bill, Individuals completing an enlistment of three years or more can receive payments / entitlements of up to 1,717.00 per month.
      • a.The monthly benefit paid is based on the:
            • 1)Type of training received.
            • 2)Member's length of service/obligation.
            • 3)Booster and NCF benefits vary individually.
      • b.Payment by direct deposit is recommended.
      • c.For Trainees on active duty, payment is limited to reimbursement of tuition and fees for the training taken.
    • 5.Pay Deductions for the Montgomery GI Bill
      • a.MGIB participants will pay $1,200 at $100 per month for the first 12 full months of active duty.
      • b.Payments into the MGIB are non-refundable.
    • 6.The purpose of the Post-9/11 GI Bill is to offer eligible participants an alternative to other educational benefit programs, such as the MGIB for active duty, the MGIB-SR (selective reserves) and the MGIB-REAP (Reserve Educational Assistance Program).
      • a.Your decision on which program to use should depend on several factors, including the type of education or training you plan to take and the amount of benefits available through each program.
      • b.This information is intended to provide you an overview of the program. Specific benefits will vary depending upon the type of school you plan to attend and the state in which it is located.
    • 7.Post-9/11 GI Bill Entitlement
      • a.Available with 90 days of active duty or more, beginning on or after 11 September 2001; or discharge with a service-connected disability after 30 consecutive days of active duty.
      • b.100 percent of maximum benefit payable with 36 months of service.
      • c.Pro-rated benefits based on qualifying active duty time.
      • d.You do not have to pay into the Post-9/11 GI Bill to become eligible.
      • e.Exceptions to qualifying active duty service
    • 8.Loan Repayment Program: Qualifying start date follows completion of 3 years of active duty.
      • a.The Post-9/11 GI Bill allows officers who graduated from service academies or received ROTC scholarships to qualify for the GI Bill benefit. However, time spent satisfying the
        • ROTC/Service Academy active duty obligation does not count toward the active duty service necessary to qualify for the benefits.
      • b.Accessions Training (BMT) and 'A' school training time is excluded if active duty service is less than 24 months.
      • c.Tuition & Fees when pursuing a college degree
      • d.Allows net payment of all in-state tuition and fees at public institutions, including graduate and professional degrees and caps private & foreign school tuition and fees at $22,805.34/year (Apr17). (See www.gibill.va.gov/resources to update payment caps)
      • e.Tuition & fees when pursing a non-college degree net payment for in-state tuition or fees for public institutions.
            • 1)$22,805.34/year (Apr17) for foreign & private schools.
            • 2)On the Job Training (OJT) pays prorated on time in the program.
            • 3)Flight training pays the actual net costs not to exceed $13,061.86 per year. (Apr17)
            • 4)Correspondence courses pays actual net costs not to exceed $11,076.86 per year. (Apr17)
    • 9.Navy College Fund (NCF)
      • a.Used to recruit some high school graduates into designated Navy jobs (ratings) designated by the Navy as "critical" or "hard-to-fill".
      • b.Must be enrolled in the MGIB program
      • c.NOT all who are eligible will be guaranteed NCF; it is awarded by the classifier, not the recruiter.
