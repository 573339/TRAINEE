C. Topic Outline

  1. Introduction
  2. The following Naval Standards will be touched on in this lesson: P436, P440, V500. NAVPERS 18068F, Naval Standards for E1 through E3 may be found in the Recruit Trainee Guide
  3. Purpose of the Military Leave and Earnings Statement-Provides a monthly record of your leave and pay account that shows:
    1. Entitlements (pay)
    2. Deductions
    3. Allotments
    4. Leave
  4. Military Pay Entitlements
    1. Pay
      1. Paid to you for services rendered.
      2. Starts first day of active duty.
      3. Three types:
        1. : Based on pay grade and length of service.
        2. : Pay you receive for certain types of duty.
        3. : Special circumstances such as reenlistment or particular type of duty.
    2. Allowances
      1. Money used to reimburse a person for expenses required to perform your job; e.g.
        1. Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS)
          1. Provides money for food.
          2. Entitlement depends on your status and availability of a government mess.
        2. Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH)
          1. BAQ and VHA combined
          2. Supplements housing costs
          3. With or without dependent rates
        3. Clothing Maintenance Allowance
          1. Initial
          2. Annual
        4. Other Allowances
          1. Family Separation Allowance (FSA)
          2. Cost of Living Allowance (COLA)
          3. Overseas Housing Allowance (OHA)
          4. Variable Housing Allowance (VHA)
      2. Allowances are not taxable
    3. Enlistment bonuses
      1. Paid upon completion of formal training27-33% income tax is withheld
        1. Lump sum
        2. Periodic installments
      2. 27-33% income tax is withheld
  5. Military Pay Deductions
    1. Federal Income Tax
      1. The amount withheld from your pay for Federal Income Tax depends on:
        1. Amount you earn
        2. Information you give on IRS form W-4.
      2. Estimate: 10-15%
    2. State Income Tax
    3. Federal Insurance Contribution Act (FICA) - Social Security
    4. FICA - Medicare
    5. Service Member’s Group Life Insurance (SGLI)
      1. .70 cents per month for 10,000 coverage for less than max coverage amounts.
      2. $29.00 per month for $400,000 maximum coverage
      3. Enrollment is optional
    6. Armed Forces Retirement Home (AFRH)
    7. Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB)
      1. Costs $100 per month for 12 months
      2. $600 contribution or “buy up”
      3. You are automatically enrolled, and must choose to disenroll. This will be explained during that class.
    8. Mid-month Pay
      1. Your LES is available on DFAS myPay at the end of each month.
      2. This section indicates the pay received at mid-month.
    9. Ditty Bag
      1. Costs approximately $500-$580 depending on Summer or Winter issue.
    10. Uniforms
      1. Cost is deducted from future pay.
      2. Recruits are financially responsible for replacing uniforms lost due to neglect.
    11. Thrift Savings Plan (TSP)
      1. An optional retirement savings and investment plan.
      2. Maximum contribution is 100% of basic pay.
      3. If contributing from basic pay, you may also contribute 1-100% of incentive or special pay.
    12. Allotments
      1. Using allotments, you may assign part of your pay to a spouse, parent, bank, etc.
      2. An allotment for child support begins on 1-1 DOT.
      3. You may start additional allotments via myPay.
  6. Direct Deposit System (DDS)/Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)
    1. Purpose - Automatically deposits your pay into your bank or credit union account.
    2. You will not receive an actual paycheck. You must select a financial institution for Direct Deposit.
    3. Your pay will be deposited directly into your account.
    4. Benefits - DDS is:
      1. - no payroll checks to lose.
      2. - No long lines at disbursing.
      3. - Dependents are taken care of during deployments.
      4. Fast - Pay is available in the members account on payday.
      5. Easy – Once enrolled, it is automatic.
  7. Procedures and Rules Governing Recruit Finances
    1. Paydays
      1. Recruits are paid be electronic funds transfer through direct deposit systems two or three times while at RTC.
      2. Navy personnel are paid on the and of each month.
      3. If the 1st or 15th is on a weekend or holiday, personnel are paid on the first preceding workday.
  8. Banks and Credit Unions
    1. Everyone needs a place to keep their money that is safe, secure and easily accessible.
      1. Banks
        1. A commercial bank is a corporation owned by (for profit).
          1. Some of the profits are reinvested in the growth and maintenance of the institution.
        2. Offers a full range of services such as:
          1. accounts
          2. Safe deposit boxes, loans
        3. Federally charted banks are insured against loss by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC).
      2. Credit Unions
        1. Credit Unions are owned by the .
          1. Members pool their deposits which are invested and lent to other members.
          2. Some of the returns on investment and lending are reinvested in the growth and maintenance of the institution.
        2. Members generally share a common affiliation, such as the same employer, labor union or association.
        3. Offers a full range of services such as:
          1. Checking, savings accounts, loans
        4. Credit Unions are insured against loss by the National Credit Union Share Insurance Fund which is administered by the Nation Credit Union Administration (NCUA).
    2. Selecting a financial institution.
      1. Choose a financial institution that has a location that will be convenient for you.
      2. Convenient hours
      3. ATM locations
      4. Services:
        1. Both Credit Unions and Banks offer a wide range of services
        2. Make sure yours offers the best services you need at a reasonable cost
        3. Initially all you may need is a checking and/or savings account
        4. After recruit training, compare cost and other services such as:
          1. Certificates of Deposit
          2. Safety Deposit Boxes
          3. Loans
  9. Checking and Savings Accounts
    1. With every dollar you earn a decision needs to be made - spend on current expenses or save for future needs.
    2. A checking account would probably be best for present day expenses.
      1. Typical present day expenses:
        1. Rent
        2. Utilities (gas/electric/water)
        3. Insurance
        4. Cell phone
        5. Recreation
        6. Car/Vehicle
      2. Can be opened as an individual or joint account.
        1. Advantages/Disadvantages
          1. account - Only you are responsible for the management of the account.
          2. account - Joint account holders bear equal responsibility for the account.
          3. Joint account makes bill paying easier, especially if you are on deployment.
          4. However, with a joint account it is more difficult to manage the account when two or more people are using the same account.
          5. You decide which meets your needs.
  10. Identify Need for Emergency Funds
    1. Savings Account
      1. Excellent place to hold your money if you don’t need it for day to day expenses.
        1. Down payment on a car
        2. Education costs
        3. Travel/vacations
        4. Retirement
      2. Savings Goals
        1. Short Term - Save $500 within 3-6 months.
        2. Secondary or Long Term Goal - Save an amount equal to 3 times your monthly expenses.
  11. Account Management
    1. Check with your bank or credit union each payday to verify the amount of your deposit either online or by phone.
    2. Record that amount in your register.
      1. In the beginning your amounts may be irregular due to your pay date and processing of your DDS paperwork.
        1. Your pay will normalize after a month or two.
    3. Remember when you write a check that is your promise to pay. Always ensure there are sufficient funds to cover that check. Don’t wait for it to clear before writing it down in your register and subtracting from your available funds.
    4. Paying Bills
      1. Contact your if payment will be late because of enlistment in the Navy.
      2. Bills can be paid by:
        1. Personal check or electronically.
          1. Ensure you have sufficient funds in your account.
          2. Your checks and debit cards will be issued to you in a couple of weeks by the representative you select today.
        2. Money Orders
      3. Later as part of your military training, you will receive instruction on how to write checks, maintain a checkbook register and checking account reconciliation.
      4. While at Recruit Training Command and your follow-on command, you have access to a Command Financial Specialist that can assist you with basic checking account management.
  12. Automatic Teller Machines
    1. ATM’s are computer terminals located in financial institutions and elsewhere accessed by your Personal Identification Number (PIN).
    2. Can be used for withdrawals, deposits and transfer of funds between accounts or other transactions that would be accomplished by a teller.
    3. You may be charged a fee for ATM transactions
      1. Make sure you the fees and deduct them from your account balance.
  13. Sign Up Procedures
    1. You will have the opportunity to meet representatives from NFCU and AFB if you so desire.
    2. Financial decisions are personal and you are free to bank with whomever you want.
    3. You can stay with your hometown provider if you choose.
    4. You must select of the three options shown on the screen.
      1. Here are some basic questions you might want to ask:
        1. How much do checks cost?
        2. What are your ATM fees?
        3. What is the fee if I write a check or use my debit/check card with insufficient funds?
    5. If you have any financial questions while here at recruit training, work with your RDC to see a Command Financial Representation.
  14. Relationship Between the Navy Core Values and Military Pay and Benefits
    1. Honor - Be honest with your finances.
    2. Courage
      1. If having financial difficulties, have the courage to talk to personnel in your chain of command.
      2. Although they cannot provide money, they can direct you to people that can help.
    3. Commitment - Be committed to:
      1. Paying bills on time
      2. Spending your money wisely
