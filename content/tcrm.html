• A.Introduction
    • We conduct dangerous or risky actions without realizing it. In the Navy, risky behavior can endanger the lives of many. Operational Risk Management (ORM) is an integral part of the decision-making process for all Navy personnel, on and off duty. Time Critical Risk Management (TCRM) refers to apply ORM at the point of commencing or during execution of a mission or task.

  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.What is Time Critical Risk Management (TCRM)?
      • a.Time Critical Risk Management (TCRM) is using risk management concepts when time and resources are limited.
        • (1)The ABCD Model is based on fundamental scientific principles; the ability to focus individuals, increase their situational awareness (SA), and improve their performance in the time critical environment.
          • (a)TCRM model consists of graphic representations or icons and the easy to remember mnemonic ABCD.
            • 1)A-Assess the situation.
            • 2)B-Balance Resources.
            • 3)C-Communicate to others.
            • 4)D-Do the event and Debrief.
        • (2)TCRM plays a role in improving our chance of completing the task or mission successfully.
      • b.The nature of TCRM decisions includes an understanding that:
        • (1)Using the ABCD Model daily creates a habit and trains the brain to continue thinking under stress or duress. The model is designed to assist when:
          • (a)Working in a changing environment.
          • (b)Monitoring a static or routine situation to capture errors.
          • (c)Making a decision with partial information.
        • (2)In all three situations, it’s necessary to develop habits that trigger the TCRM process to “Assess”, “Balance”, “Communicate”, “Do and Debrief” the event continuously as necessary.
    • 2.The ABCD Model of Risk Management
      • a.The visual reminder or ABCD (icon) assists in the recall of the concepts and the principles behind the ABCD’s and improves communications during the event.
      • b.The model provides a common language and structure for a measured response when an individual, team, or crew is executing a routine task, or when under stress from a more complex situation.
        • (1)
          • (a)In the execution of a task or mission, where time and resources are most limited, an individual must be proactive and responsive to ensure success.
          • (b)The ABCD model establishes a structure for standardized thought and communication while executing a task.
          • (c)Using the ABCD model daily will result in deep memory of those learned qualities.
          • (d)When executing tasks, leadership and team members have an expectation of a response similar to their experience.
        • (2)Assess the situation - In a real-time situation, it is essential to consider the event.
          • (a)Are things going as planned?
          • (b)What’s different from the plan?
          • (c)What’s confusing me?
          • (d)What will happen next?
      • c.Situational Awareness (SA)
        • (1)Situational Awareness refers to the degree of accuracy by which your perception of the current environment mirrors reality.
          • (a)What’s going on?
          • (b)What’s going to happen next?
          • (c)Real risk vs. perceived risk.
      • d.Situational Awareness-Risk.
        • (1)Risk is gauged in three colors:
          • (a)Green -- Little chance of serious error. Errors may be made but should be caught by the individual.
          • (b)Yellow -- Higher chance of serious error. Stressed or less focused on task and surroundings. Errors possibly not caught.
          • (c)Red -- Very high chance of serious error. Error that cannot be caught.
          • (d)Swooshing Arrow -- Return to good situation awareness.
        • (2)The target is used during team communication to focus on one or more individuals on an understanding of situational conditions or an individual can use it as a self-assessment.
          • (a)Good communication is essential to risk management.
        • (3)The blocks represent multiple levels of resources (i.e., policies, procedures, processes, checklists, automation, briefings, external resources, knowledge, skills, techniques, etc...)
        • (4)The ABCD block represents the TCRM mnemonic.
          • (a)Assess the situation - In a real-time situation, it is essential to consider the event.
          • (b)Balance Resources - Make the risk decisions to mitigate risks. Prior planning ensures more resources are available.
          • (c)Communicate to others - Ensure everyone knows what is expected and what could go wrong. Communicate while the event is going on to help everyone maintain SA.
          • (d)Do and Debrief - Do the mission and use the appropriate resources to mitigate risk.
      • e.ABCD model provides a common language and structure for a measured response when an individual, team or crew is executing a routine task, or when under duress from a more complex situation resulting from added conditions, crew factors or task loading.
        • (1)Training to the ABCD Model will embed a set of patterns that will help you recognize and recall a set of actions to counter risk even when distracted.
        • (2)This simple and easy to remember mnemonic provides a means to evaluate risk and formulate mitigation strategies on-the-run for both on- and off-duty situations.
      • f.Time critical decision making requires a unique set of skills, which must be practiced.
        • (1)TCRM uses the decision maker's previous experience, training, and availability to recall available resources.
        • (2)Time critical decisions are based on pattern matching to past training and experience, and the recall of resources available in the ABCD model format.
        • (3)Using TCRM, personnel Assess the situation, Balance their resources, Communicate to all concerned, and Do some action to complete the mission/task or mitigate risk by acting on the condition, then follow-up by Debriefing the results to feedback lessons learned.
        • (4)Use of standardized communication structure during time critical evolutions reduces conflicts and errors.
