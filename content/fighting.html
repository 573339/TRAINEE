A. Introduction

Since everyone aboard ship is a potential firefighter, it is essential that all personnel know the procedures to report a fire, the procedures to follow when a fire alarm is sounded, and their roles when fighting the fire, the procedures to follow when a fire alarm is sounded, and their roles when fighting the fire. When your ship is on fire, you are fighting for the lives of yourself, your shipmates, and the existence of your ship.

    1. Report the fire, by the fastest means, such as messenger or sound-powered telephone, to the OOD.
    2. Give the following information:
      1. of casualty -- What class of fire; e.g., color of smoke and location.

    3. If the fire is small (e.g., fire in trashcan), attempt to extinguish it. By this time, help should be on the way.
    4. If the fire threatens to get out of control before the repair party arrives, try to prevent it from spreading by:

        1. First, tighten the dogs opposite the hinges with enough pressure to hold the door closed.
        2. Set dogs snug on hinge side of door.
        3. Tighten all dogs evenly using an “X” pattern.

    5. If the fire gets out of control, evacuate all personnel.
  3. Methods of Sounding the Alarm for a Fire Aboard Ship
    1. At sea, a fire alarm can be given over the ship’s 1MC announcing system by any of the following ways:
      1. alarm sounded followed by a message telling location of the fire.
      2. Using the ship’s and the 1MC general announcing system in the following sequence:
        1. Rapid ringing of the ships bell.
        2. Pause.
        3. One, two, or three rings to indicate to location of the fire.
          1. One ring -- .
          2. Two rings -- amidships.
          3. Three rings -- .
        4. Pause.
        5. General announcement giving the exact location of the fire.
        6. Pause.
        7. Repeat same sequence; that is, repeat paragraphs (a) through (e).
    2. In port, a fire alarm is given by the rapid ringing of ship’s bell and the ship’s 1MC system -- as at sea.
  4. Procedures for Responding to a Shipboard Fire Alarm
    1. When a fire alarm is sounded at sea:
      1. The fire party (also known as the flying squad) responds to the casualty.
      2. If the fire is not quickly contained, _ is sounded and the ship’s crew responds.
    2. When a fire alarm is sounded in port, the duty in port fire party proceeds to the scene and fights the fire.
    3. Designated fire party personnel report to their repair locker to don battle dress and required protective gear.

  6. Fire Party Procedures for Fighting a Fire
    1. Fire party reports to scene
    2. On-scene telephone talker establishes communication with repair locker.
    3. Isolate the fire -- close all doors, hatches and .
    4. Set fire boundaries in surrounding compartments.
      1. Remove combustible materials.
      2. Station hose personnel at fire boundaries to cool bulkheads and decks.
    5. The electrician will secure power when ordered by the on-scene leader. Electrical power supplied to areas affected by the fire should be secured.
    6. Break out and man hoses. OSL reports manned and ready to the repair locker using the sound powered telephone talker.
    7. OSL orders team to activate SCBAs and set their timers. Reports time to repair locker.
    8. Charge hoses when ordered by attack team leader.
    9. Attack team leader orders nozzlemen to test extinguishing agent.
    10. Accessman checks door for heat. Reports hot or cool door to attack team leader.
    11. Accessman opens door when ordered by attack team leader.
    12. OSL makes report to repair locker of smoke/fire.
    13. Attack team leader takes charge of hose teams and enters space.
    14. Combat fire from best possible position to protect personnel.
    15. Rig portable pumps -- used if firemain pressure is lost; e.g., P-100.
    16. Keep investigating the surrounding area until fire is extinguished.
    17. OSL reports to repair locker when fire is:
      1. Contained
      2. Out
    18. Attack team leader orders setting of reflash watch. OSL reports to repair locker that reflash what is set.
    19. OSL orders:
      1. Hose teams to back out of the space.
      2. Fireplugs to be secured.
  7. Wild Fire Hose
    1. Occasionally a fire team will lose control of a fire hose or an unmanned hose will be inadvertently charged causing a wild fire hose. There are three methods of regaining control.
      1. Secure water flow at the .
      2. Make a at a nonmoving point on the hose (1 ½ hose only).
      3. Advance along hose from a nonmoving point toward the nozzle.
  8. Relationship between Teamwork and a Fire Party Extinguishing a Fire
    1. Damage control central directs all personnel involved in containing and extinguishing the fire. They ensure that all personnel work as a team.
    2. The on-scene leader, attack team leader, nozzleman, hosemen/plugman, and accessman work as a team to access and extinguish the fire.
    3. Boundary men and investigators work to contain the fire.
    4. The electrician, dewatering/desmoking team, agent supply men, sound-powered telephone talkers, and messengers work to support the firefighting effort.
    5. To work as a team and extinguish the fire, good communication between all members of the fire party is vital.
  9. Relationship between the Navy Core Values and Extinguishing a Fire
    1. Courage -- Personnel may have to display Courage to fight a fire when their life and ship are in danger; e.g., the Forrestal and Stark incidents.
    2. Commitment -- Personnel must be committed to perform their duties, as a member of the fire party, to the best of their abilities.
