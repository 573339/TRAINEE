• A.Introduction
    • Portable damage control pumps are used to fight fires and dewater flooded spaces to keep the ship stable and return the space to use. This lesson discusses the pumps used to accomplish this.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • IDENTIFY the purpose and characteristics of the portable damage control pumps and eductors
    their components.
    EXPLAIN the relationship between the Navy Core Values and portable damage control pumps.
    EXPLAIN the relationship between teamwork and portable damage control pumps.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.P-100 Portable Pump
      • a.Purpose
        • (1)Primary -- Supplies water for firefighting.
        • (2)Secondary – dewater flooded spaces.
      • b.Components and accessories
        • (1)Fuel tank -- Contains JP-5 or F-76 fuel to operate the pump.
        • (2)Suction hose (3”) -- Used to draw water into the pump.
        • (3)Discharge valve (2½” outlet) -- Allows water to flow from the pump.
        • (4)Exhaust hose -- Carries exhaust fumes to outside atmosphere.
      • c.Safety precautions
        • (1)Exhaust gases contain carbon monoxide (CO).
          • (a)Carbon Monoxide will kill if inhaled for more than a few minutes.
          • (b)When pump is used inside the ship, exhaust gases must be vented safely to the atmosphere.
        • (2)Before operating the pump, ensure exhaust hose connections are properly secured.
    • 2.Portable Eductors
      • a.Purpose -- Used for dewatering flooded spaces.
      • b.Comes in two models:
        • (1)Single jet (S-type) -- Will not pass debris due to the attached strainer basket.
        • (2)Perijet -- Will pass debris up to 2½ inches in diameter.
      • c.Accessories
        • (1)Supply hose -- 2½ inch hose used to supply water to the eductor.
        • (2)Discharge hose -- 4 inch hose used for eductor discharge overboard.
      • d.Operation
        • (1)An eductor has no moving parts.
        • (2)Water supplied through the supply hose by the firemain system or P-100 pump passes quickly through the eductor to create a vacuum.
        • (3)The vacuum causes the water from the flooded space to flow through the intake into the eductor.
        • (4)The water then passes through the discharge hose and flows overboard.
    • 3.Portable Electric Submersible Pump
      • a.Purpose
        • (1)Used to dewater flooded spaces.
        • (2)Safety Precaution: Electrical submersible pump motors are cooled by the fluid passing through the pump casing and circulating around the motor; therefore, they should NOT be used to pump:
          • (a)Gasoline -- Heat generated by the pump motor could cause an explosion.
          • (b)Heavy oil and hot water -- Could burn out electric motor.
      • b.Components and Accessories
        • (1)Handle -- Used to hand-carry pump; removable.
        • (2)On/off electrical switch.
        • (3)Strainer -- Keeps debris from clogging pump.
        • (4)Discharge hose (2½ inch) -- Carries water overboard.
        • (5)Tending line -- Used to raise and lower the pump. Do NOT use the electric cable to raise and lower the pump.
    • 4.Relationship between the Navy Core Values and Portable Damage Control Pump
      • Commitment -- Be Committed to:
      • a.Learning information about portable damage control pumps to facilitate job performance.
      • b.Operating portable damage control pumps according to proper procedures.
    • 5.Relationship between Teamwork and Portable Damage Control Pumps
      • a.Personnel must work together as a team to transport, set up and operate portable damage control pumps.
