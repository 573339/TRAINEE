• A.Introduction
    • It is very important to be aware of legal protections afforded you by your status as an active duty Servicemember under the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act of 2003 (50 U.S.C. App. §§501-597b).
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • IDENTIFY protections offered under the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act of 2003.
    EXPLAIN the relationship between the Navy Core Values and Servicemembers Civil Relief Act
    of 2003.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Purpose of the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) of 2003 - Provides servicemembers relief from certain civil obligations and temporarily suspends judicial and administrative proceedings and transactions involving civil liabilities when military service materially affects the ability of a servicemember to meet or attend to civil matters.
      • a.Why do we have the SCRA?
        • (1)“Provide for, strengthen, and expedite the national defense through protection extended to servicemembers of the United States to enable such persons to devote their entire energy to the defense needs of the Nation”.
        • (2)To “provide for the temporary suspension of judicial and administrative proceedings and transactions that may adversely affect the civil rights of servicemembers during their military service”.
      • b.Whom does it protect?
        • (1)Active duty servicemembers
        • (2)Dependents of active duty servicemembers
        • (3)Persons also responsible for obligations
      • c.Who must comply?
        • (1)Federal Government.
        • (2)States, territories, and local governments.
        • (3)Private individuals and businesses.
        • (4)U.S. Courts, but...SCRA is NOT protection from criminal action.
      • d.How can you benefit from SCRA?
        • (1)Civil court matters
          • (a)Protection against default judgment
          • (b)You have 90 days after release from active duty to reopen case.
          • (c)Stay or delay of civil proceedings:
          • (d)While on active duty.
          • (e)Within 90 days after release from active duty.
          • (f)You must make application.
        • (2)6% interest on pre-service debts.
          • (a)Debt incurred prior to entering active service.
          • (b)Loan payments must be reamortized.
          • (c)Includes interest, service charges, renewal charges, fee, or any valid charges. All such additional charges combined cannot exceed the 6% annual rate.
          • (d)You must inform your creditors in writing and provide a copy of military orders. You have up to 180 days after release from active duty to apply.
          • (e)6% cap is applied from entire time in service (+1 year).
          • (f)Additional interest is not repaid.
          • (g)6% cap does NOT apply to federal guaranteed student loan.
          • (h)Courts can determine interest in excess of 6% is not materially affected by reason of servicemember’s military service.
        • (3)Rental Eviction
          • (a)Without a court order, a landlord may not evict a servicemember on active duty or their dependents where:
            • 1)The premises are occupied or intended as a primary residence; and
            • 2)The monthly payments are less than $2,465 (2004) (adjusted annually for CPI housing component);
            • 3)Regardless of whether the premises were rented before or after the members entry onto active duty;
          • (b)The court may adjust the rental payments in the interest of the parties.
          • (c)A landlord’s violation of this provision of the SCRA may be a criminal misdemeanor offense which is punishable by a sentence of up to one year imprisonment.
        • (4)Installment Contracts
          • (a)SCRA protects servicemembers who have contracted for the purchase, lease, or bailment of real personal property (e.g., motor vehicle) prior to the time they entered military service.
            • 1)Provided that a deposit or installment payment has already been paid by the servicemember on obligation before entering military service.
          • (b)A creditor CANNOT:
            • 1)Rescind or terminate an installment contract for breach of terms without a court order.
            • 2)Repossess property purchased, leased, or bailed by a servicemember without a court order.
          • (c)A violation of this provision of the SCRA may be a criminal misdemeanor offence which is punishable by a sentence of up to one year imprisonment.
            • 1)It will also subject the creditor to damages for wrongful conversion of property if there is no court order.
        • (5)Mortgage Protection
          • (a)The SCRA protects servicemembers obligations on real or personal property that is secured by a mortgage, trust deed, or similar security.
            • 1)The obligation must have been incurred before the period of military service.
            • 2)The property must still be owned by the member, and;
            • 3)The alleged failure to meet the secured obligation must have been occurred before or during the military service.
        • (6)Lease Terminations
          • (a)The SCRA permits a servicemember on active duty in excess of 90 days to lawfully terminate a lease without penalty. The member should provide written notice to their landlord of their intent to terminate the lease. Notice should include a copy of orders proving the lease was signed prior to coming on active duty.
          • (b)The SCRA expressly extends this protection to the lease of automobiles where the period of active duty of at least 180 days begins during the lease term.
          • (c)This portion also provides for the termination of an automobile lease on the occasion of the member receiving PCS orders to an overseas location.
      • e.Civil court cases you don’t know about
        • (1)Civil court appoints a lawyer
          • (a)Track you down.
          • (b)Seek a delay of case for you if you do not have a defense or cannot be found.
        • (2)If you lose because you failed to appear in court
          • (a)You MAY have 90 days after release from active duty to reopen the case.
        • (3)You can ask the court to delay the case by:
          • (a)Submitting a letter saying why military duties prevent you from defending yourself and stating a date when you can defend yourself AND
          • (b)Submitting a letter from your Commanding Officer saying why military duties prevent you from defending yourself and stating that leave will not be granted now.
        • (4)Court must grant you at least 90 days
      • f.Other protections offered
        • (1)Preservation of some insurance policies.
        • (2)Delay payment of certain income taxes.
        • (3)Preservation of legal residence for tax and voting rights.
        • (4)You may waive your rights under any SCRA provision.
      • g.For further information, go to NKO or contact military legal assistance office.
