A. Introduction

U.S. Navy uniforms are outward symbols of outward tradition and professionalism. Sailors must comply with Uniform regulations and grooming standards to reflect a positive Navy image.

  2. Purpose of the United States Navy Uniform Regulations
    1. Provides:
      1. Descriptions of all U.S. Navy uniforms and their components.
      2. Guidance to prescribe uniform wear.
    2. Carries the force of a general order.
  3. Care, Cleaning, and Replacement of Navy Uniforms
    1. Washing, dry cleaning, alterations, and repairs are the member’s responsibility.
    2. Follow manufacturer’s labels inside uniform items for proper cleaning requirements.
    3. Uniforms shall be kept clean; lace, devices, and insignia must be kept free of tarnish and corrosion.
    4. No articles shall protrude from or be visible on the uniform; e.g., pens, key chains, combs, cigarettes, papers, cellular phones, etc. Protruding objects detract from a professional military appearance and could distort the uniform shape.
    5. Uniforms should be stored on hangers. If uniforms are to be stored for a long time, they should be cleaned and packed in an airtight plastic bag with a packet of desiccant (drying agent).
    6. The Clothing Replacement Allowance (CRA) is an annual cash payment provided for replacing a minimum quantity of each required uniform.
  4. Grooming Standards
    1. While in uniform, personal appearance will be neat and professional.
    2. Hair shall be kept neat, clean, and well groomed.
      1. Requirements ensure that hair does not interfere with proper wearing of military headgear and safety equipment.
      2. Faddish styles and outrageous multicolored hair are NOT authorized.
        1. Faddish are followed for a short time to an exaggerated level. They are generally conspicuous and detract from a professional appearance.
        2. Examples include:
          1. Men - Haircut with a distinct visible line (bowl cut or high and tight).
          2. Women - Extremely short, crop-cut hair (GI Jane).
      3. Varying hairstyles, including Afros, are permitted providing criteria of maximum length, bulk, and tapered neck and sides are met.
      4. Hairpieces may be worn only for cosmetic reasons to cover natural baldness or physical disfigurement.
        1. The will present a natural appearance and conform to all grooming standards.
        2. They may not interfere with the performance of duty or present a safety or foreign object damage (FOD) hazard.
    3. Hair standards for men
      1. Hair above the ears and around the neck shall be tapered upward at least ¾” and no more than ¾” outward to blend with hairstyle.
      2. Hair shall be no longer than four inches and must NOT:
        1. Touch the top of the collar.
        2. Extend below the eyebrows.
        3. Be plaited (tightly woven hair braided close to the scalp) or braided while in uniform or duty status.
        4. Exceed two inches in bulk (the distance that the mass of hair protrudes from the scalp).
      3. The unique quality and texture of curled, kinked, waved, and straight hair are recognized. Sometimes the ¾” taper at the back of the neck may be difficult to attain; in these cases:
        1. Hair must present a graduated appearance.
        2. The taper may combine with a line at the back of the neck.
      4. One natural, fore and aft, narrow part may be cut, clipped, or shaved into the hair.
      5. Men may shave their heads if trimmed every day. Men may NOT shave their heads for:
        1. Faddish reasons.
        2. Affiliation with an extremist group.
      6. Keep sideburns trimmed and tailored in the same manner as the haircut. They shall:
        1. Not extend below a point level with the middle of the ear.
        2. Be of even width (not flared).
        3. End with a clean-shaven horizontal line.
      7. Mustaches will be kept neatly and closely trimmed.
        1. No portion of the mustache shall extend below the line of the upper lip.
        2. A mustache shall NOT go:
          1. Beyond a horizontal line extending across the corners of the mouth.
          2. More than ¼” beyond a vertical line drawn from the corner of the mouth.
        3. Handlebar mustaches, goatees, beards, or eccentricities are NOT permitted.
      8. A beard may only be worn for medical reasons when authorized by the Commanding Officer on the advice of a medical officer.
    4. Hair standards for women
      1. When wearing collared uniforms, the hair may touch, but not fall below a horizontal line level with the lower edge of the back of the collar.
      2. When wearing the jumper uniform (crackerjacks), hair may extend a maximum of 1½” below the top of the jumper collar.
      3. Hair shall not show under the front of the brim of the:
        1. Combination hat
        2. Garrison cap
        3. Eight Point Cover
        4. Navy/Command ball cap
      4. Hairstyles with multiple braids may be worn; corn rows, French braids, etc.).
        1. Braids shall be approximately ¼” in diameter and tightly woven.
        2. Foreign material, such as beads and decorative items, shall not be braided into the hair.
        3. Ponytails, pigtails, and widely spaced, individual hanging locks are not permitted.
      5. Ponytails, pigtails, and widely spaced, individual hanging locks are not permitted.
      6. Appropriateness of hairstyle is also nudged by its appearance when headgear is worn.
      7. Long hair, including braids, must:
        1. Be neatly and inconspicuously fastened, pinned, or secured to the head and may not dangle free at any point.
        2. Not be lopsided or extremely asymmetrical.
      8. The bulk of the hair shall not exceed two inches.
      9. A maximum of 2 small barrettes, combs, or similar to hair color may be used.
      10. Fabric elastics (scrunchies), beads, and colored rubber bands/pins are not authorized.
      11. Hair may not be outrageously multi-colored or faddish, including shaved portions (except neckline), or have designs cut or braided into it.
      12. Wigs or hairpieces, meeting grooming standards, may be worn.
    5. Fingernails shall be well manicured and clean at all times.
      1. For men, fingernails shall NOT extend past the fingertips.
      2. For women:
        1. Fingernails shall NOT exceed ¼” measured from the fingertips.
        2. Nail polish may be worn, but shall be conservative and compliment the skin tone.
    6. Conservative jewelry is authorized for all personnel but must be in good taste. It shall not present a safety or FOD (Foreign Object Damage) hazard.
      1. Necklaces
        1. Authorized for and .
        2. While in uniform, only one necklace may be worn and must NOT be visible.
      2. Wristwatches and bracelets
        1. Authorized for men and women.
        2. While in uniform, only one of each may be worn.
        3. Ankle bracelets are not authorized while in uniform.
      3. Rings
        1. For men and women while in uniform, only one ring per hand is authorized, plus a wedding ring or engagement/wedding ring set.
        2. They are NOT authorized for wear on thumbs.
      4. Earrings
        1. For men are NOT authorized:
          1. When in uniform.
          2. In civilian attire when in a duty status; aboard any ship, craft, or aircraft; in a military vehicle, or other place under military jurisdiction.
          3. While participating in any organized military recreational activity.
          4. When prohibited by prescribing authority while in foreign countries. (Prescribing authority is a person in command of a geographical area; responsible for issuing policy for wearing Navy uniforms.)
        2. For women:
          1. One earring per ear (centered on the earlobe) may be worn while in uniform.
          2. Earrings shall be silver 4-6mm (1/8”-1/4”) ball.
            1. Plain with brushed matte or gold finish.
            2. Screw-on or with posts.
    7. NO articles, other than earrings for women, shall be attached through a body part.
    8. Having one’s body pierced is NOT authorized:
      1. While in uniform.
      2. When in civilian attire while on duty, aboard a ship, craft, aircraft, or in a military vehicle.
      3. Within any base or other place under military jurisdiction.
      4. While participating in any organized military recreational activities.
    9. Intentional body mutilation, piercing, branding, scarring that is excessive or eccentric is prohibited.
      1. For example:
        1. Split or forked tongue.
        2. Foreign objects inserted under the skin to create a design or pattern.
        3. Enlarged or stretched out holes in the ears, other than normal piercing.
        4. Intentional scarring on the neck, face, or scalp.
      2. Waivers may be requested for existing body alterations.
    10. Coloring teeth for ornamental purposes is prohibited.
      1. For example:
        1. Gold
        2. Platinum
      2. Teeth will not be ornamented with caps, designs, jewels, initials, etc.
      3. Waivers may be requested for existing dental ornamentation.
    11. Cosmetics (women only), if worn, shall be applied in good taste so that colors blend with natural skin tome and enhance natural features. Exaggerated or faddish cosmetic styles are not authorized with the uniform shall NOT be worn.
  5. Proper Wear of Enlisted (E-1 through E-6) Uniforms and Related Items Many of the uniforms worn by E-1 through E-6 are also worn by Officers and Chief Petty Officers. When greeting a senior military person, pay attention to the rank insignia worn on their uniform, not the style of uniform.
    1. Dinner dress blue
      1. Blue jumper or blue coat and skirt with miniature medals.
      2. White hat or combination cap.
      3. Outer garments: Peacoat or all-weather coat.
      4. Occasion for wear: Official functions where civilians wear a black tie or evening gown. This is a prescribable winter uniform.
    2. Dinner dress white
      1. White jumper with miniature medals. Females may wear skirt or slacks.
      2. White hat or combination cap.
      3. Occasion for wear: Official functions where civilians wear a black tie or evening gown. This may be prescribed as a summer uniform.
    3. Full dress blue
      1. Blue jumper or blue coat and skirt or slacks.
      2. White hat or combination cap.
      3. Outer garments: Peacoat or all-weather coat.
      4. Occasion for wear: Change of command, official visits, ceremonies, and solemn occasions.
      5. Wear both large medals and ribbons that do not have corresponding large medals on Full Dress Uniforms.
        1. Large medals are worn on the left breast of the service coat or jumper of Full Dress Uniforms.
        2. Center ribbons on the right breast in the same relative position as the holding bar of the lowest row of medals. Arrange ribbons in order of precedence in rows from top down and inboard to outboard.
    4. Full dress white
      1. White jumper with large medals and ribbons. Females may wear skirt or slacks.
      2. White hat or combination hat.
      3. Occasion for wear: Change of command, official visits, ceremonies, and solemn occasions.
    5. Service dress blue
      1. Blue jumper or blue coat and skirt or slacks with ribbons.
      2. White hat, combination cap, or beret. Females may wear the garrison cap only when wearing the black V-neck sweater in place of the blue coat.
      3. Outer garments: Pullover or all-weather coat.
        1. Men may wear the pullover jersey under the blue jumper.
        2. Women may wear the black V-neck sweater or cardigan in place of the blue coat.
        3. When appropriate for civilians to wear dress and .
        4. Black V-neck sweater may not be worn for ceremonies or high level meetings. Cardigans are worn only in immediate work area.
    6. Service dress white
      1. White jumper with ribbons. Females may wear skirt or slacks.
      2. White hat, combination cap, or beret.
      3. Outer garments: All-Weather Coat or Peacoat.
      4. Occasion for wear: In summer when dinner dress and full dress uniforms are not prescribed and civilian dress is coat and tie.
    7. Navy Service Uniform (NSU) (Male)
      1. Short-sleeved Khaki shirt with ribbons, and miniature silver anodized collar devices (E-2 through E-6), black trousers with black belt and silver buckle.
      2. Garrison cap.
      3. Outer garments: Peacoat, all-weather coat, black (Eisenhower-style) jacket with knit stand up collar and, black V-neck sweater, or cardigan.
      4. Occasion for wear: year-round when prescribed as the uniform of the day for:
        1. Office work.
        2. Watchstanding.
        3. Liberty.
        4. Business ashore.
    8. Navy Service Uniform (NSU) (Female)
      1. Short-sleeved Khaki shirt with ribbons, and miniature silver anodized collar devices (E-2 through E-6), beltless black trousers or black skirt.
      2. Garrison cap or beret.
      3. Outer garments: Peacoat, all-weather coat, black relaxed fit jacket (with pointed collar and epaulets), black V-neck sweater.
      4. Occasion for wear: Year-round when prescribed as the uniform of the day for:
        1. Office work
        2. Watchstanding
        3. Liberty
        4. Business ashore
    9. Physical Training Uniform (PTU)
      1. Gold short or long-sleeved shirt with Navy embroidered on the front and back. Blue shorts with Navy embroidery.
      2. Tailored for a progressive fit and appearance for all sailors, E-1 through O-10. Made of modern, moisture wicking and odor-resistant fabric.
      3. Manner of wear:Designed primarily for group/unit PT activities and semi-annual Physical Fitness Assessment (PFA). It can be worn on and off base for fitness and/or leisure unless determined otherwise by Regional Coordinators or Commanding Officers.
        1. In port:
          1. PTU shirt will be tucked into shorts.
          2. Shorts will be worn fully on the waist with the length not extending below the top of the knee.
          3. Female sailors will wear a sport or dress brassiere when wearing the PTU.
          4. Running shoes and athletic socks must be worn and shall not extend above mid-calf.
        2. At sea:
          1. Wearing of the PTU will be at the discretion of the Commanding Officer.
        3. During Individual PT:
          1. Shirts may be worn in or out of shorts.
          2. All other requirements are the same as "In Port"
        4. While on liberty:
          1. Components of the PTU are authorized to wear individually or collectively.
          2. The PTU will not be worn while in a duty status or when conducting official business on base (e.g., PSD’s, medical treatment facilities, galleys, legal).
    10. Navy Working Uniform (NWU)
      1. Navy working trousers and Navy working shirt:
        1. Two cloth embroidered tapes are sewn on the shirt above the pockets:
          1. "U.S. Navy" on the wearer’s left.
          2. Wearer’s name on the right.
        2. An embroidered name tape also goes on the trousers above the wearer’s right rear pocket.
      2. Eight Point Cover
        1. Worn square on head (not resting on ears).
        2. Visor just above eye level and parallel to deck.
      3. Outer garments: coat, parka (w/Black Fleece Liner).
      4. Occasion for wear
        1. When other uniforms would be unsafe or become unduly soiled.
        2. May be authorized by prescribing authority for commuting to and from work. Commuting is defined as a direct route from place of residence to place of work by means of a private vehicle.
        3. T-shirt and mock neck sweater:
          1. Collar should fit comfortably around neck.
          2. Mock neck sweater sleeve length shall not extend beyond the length of the NWU shirt sleeve.
        4. Trousers worn at waistline with belt.
    11. Coveralls
      1. One-piece combination of overalls and shirt worn as an outer garment.
      2. Ball cap or knit (watch) cap.
      3. Outer garment: All-weather coat, blue working jacket, peacoat.
      4. Occasion for wear:
        1. When other uniforms would be unsafe or become unduly soiled.
        2. Limited to an underway uniform (i.e. from stationing and securing Sea Detail/Maneuvering watch when leaving and entering port respectively).
    12. Uniform Related Items
      1. Bags (e.g., gym bags, backpacks, garment bags, etc.) shall be hand-carried to facilitate saluting.
        1. Backpacks may be worn over the left shoulder or both shoulders while wearing service and working uniforms. Gym bags may be carried on the back when riding on a bicycle or motorcycle, but shall be hand-carried before mounting and upon dismounting.
          1. Authorized colors of backpacks: Black, navy blue, and when available the matching NWU pattern (blue/grey black).
          2. The matching NWU pattern back pack is only authorized for wear with the NWU.
          3. No personal ornamentation shall be attached on or to the backpack.
        2. Full seabags may be carried/worn on the back at any time.
        3. Only a woman’s Navy certified handbag shall be carried on the shoulder while in uniform. It shall be carried over the left shoulder or forearm with the top of the bag at waist level.
      2. Identification Cards
        1. The military ID card is the property of the U.S. and must be carried at all times.
        2. Do NOT surrender your ID card except as required by proper military authority for identification, investigation, or while in disciplinary confinement.
      3. ID Tags
        1. Prescribed part of the uniform and must be kept in your possession.
        2. Designed for the identification of members who become casualties.
        3. Are mot worn under normal situations.
      4. Sunglasses
        1. If worn while in uniform, must be conservative. Faddish or distracting styles are unauthorized (e.g., bright colors, extra-large or mirrored lenses, etc.
        2. Are never worn while in formation.
        3. Retainer straps are only worn if they are required for safety purposes. If worn, they shall be plain black and worn snugly against the back of the head.
  6. Regulations for Wearing Civilian Clothing, Drug Depiction, and Tattoos
    1. Civilian clothing is permitted when authorized by the Commanding Officer in situations such as when:
      1. Leaving or returning to ships or stations.
      2. On authorized leave of absences.
      3. Leaving or returning to ships or stations.
      4. On authorized leave of absences.
      5. On liberty.
      6. In off duty status ashore.
    2. Commands are authorized to suspend the privilege of civilian clothing for individuals:
      1. Who do not wear civilian clothing as outlined in the United States Navy Uniform Regulations,
      2. Who fail to properly maintain uniforms or seabag requirements,
      3. Whose appearance may bring discredit upon the Navy.
    3. Casual civilian clothing is only authorized on base for occasions such as picnics, athletic events, and other casual daytime activities. Otherwise, they are NOT acceptable within the confines of military installations. For example:
      1. Tank-top shirts
      2. White undershirts worn as outer garments
      3. Cut-off shorts
      4. Shower sandals
    4. Wearing or displaying clothing, jewelry, or tattoos depicting marijuana or any other controlled substance(s) or advocating drug use is prohibited.
    5. Military uniform articles that may be worn with civilian clothing include:
      1. Command ball cap (without insignia)
      2. Belts (with civilian buckles)
      3. Gloves
      4. Socks/hosiery
      5. Shoes
      6. Underwear
      7. All-weather coat (without insignia)
      8. Black relaxed fit jacket (without insignia)
      9. Sweaters (black V-neck, pullover jersey and cardigan) (without nametag)
      10. Watch cap
      11. Handbag
    6. Navy policies on tattoos, body art, and brands
      1. The Navy prohibits tattoos, body are, and brands:
        1. On the head, face, neck, scalp and arms below the elbow.
        2. That are prejudicial to good: order, discipline, and moral.
        3. That bring discredit to the Navy.
      2. Examples of prohibited tattoos, body art, and brands include those that:
        1. Depict drug use.
        2. Are obscene or sexually explicit.
        3. Symbolize discrimination, gang affiliation, or supremacist or extremist groups.
        4. Are visible through uniform clothing.
      3. Waivers may be requested from the Chief of Naval Operations for existing tattoos.
  7. Relationship between the Navy Core Values, and Grooming Standards and the Proper Wear of Uniforms
    1. Honor
      1. Be accountable for your personal appearance in uniform and civilian clothes.
      2. Be honest and truthful when reprimanded for uniform violations, vice making excuses and blaming others.
    2. Commitment - Be committed to professionalism by:c
      1. Studying the Uniform Regulations to understand the requirements.
      2. Obeying Navy uniform and grooming regulations.
  8. The Effects of Teamwork on Proper Grooming and Uniform Standards -- Working together to enforce and promote uniform and grooming standards is necessary in insure a positive image for the Navy team.
