  2. Definition and Purpose of the Thrift Savings Plan (TSP)
    1. Definition -- An optional savings and plan open to military and federal civilian personnel.
    2. Purpose -- Provides income to supplement payments from the Navy and federal retirement systems.
  3. Characteristics of the Thrift Savings Plan
    1. Offers participants the same type of savings and tax benefits that many corporations offer under 401(k) retirement plans.
    2. TSP contributions and earnings.
      1. Traditional TSP contributions and are not taxed until the funds are withdrawn.
      2. Roth TSP contributions are and earnings are not taxed when the funds are withdrawn.
    3. Payroll deduction must be used to contribute into TSP.
    4. As of 2015, you may contribute a minimum of one percent of your base pay up to a maximum of $17,500 per year.
    5. You may also contribute from:
      1. Incentive Pay
      2. Special Pay
      3. Bonuses
    6. Existing retirement accounts may be transferred or rolled over into the TSP.
    7. Reservists that are Federal Civil Service employees can have both civilian and military accounts.
    8. The Secretary of the Navy may designate personnel in critical ratings to receive matching contributions into TSP.
    9. In-Service withdrawals are allowed for participants who can document financial hardship.
      1. Before age 59.5, you will pay income tax and an early withdrawal .
      2. For information on TSP withdrawals go to www.tsp.gov or call 1-877-968-3778.
    10. Members can borrow money against their accounts.
      1. For information on TSP loans go to www.tsp.gov or call 1-877-968-3778.
    11. Upon separation from the Navy, there are several options for your TSP account:
      1. Receive a single payment for your entire account.
      2. Receive monthly payments based on the amount accumulated and one’s life expectancy.
      3. Use your account balance to purchase a TSP annuity, which pays a monthly benefit for life.
      4. Transfer TSP funds to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA) or other qualified retirement plan.
      5. Leave money in the TSP to continue earning interest.
        1. No further contributions are allowed.
        2. Withdrawal of funds must begin no later than age 70.5.
  4. Thrift Savings Plan Investment Funds and Their Characteristics
    1. Thrift Savings Plan participants can invest into any of the following funds.
      1. G (U.S. Government)
      2. F (Fixed-income Index Investment)
      3. C (Common Stock Index)
      4. S (Small Capitalization Stock Index)
      5. I (International Stock Index)
      6. L (Lifecycle)
    2. G Fund
      1. Description of Investments: Government .
      2. Objective of fund: Interest income without risk of loss of .
      3. Risk: Risk of .
      4. Volatility: Low Type of earnings: Interest
      5. The G Fund provides a steady rate of return.
    3. F Fund
      1. Description of Investments: Government, , and mortgage-backed .
      2. Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Barclays Capital U.S. Aggregate Bond Index.
      3. Risk: Market risk, Credit risk, Prepayment risk, Inflation risk.
      4. Volatility: Low to moderate Type of earnings: Change in market prices, Interest
    4. C Fund
      1. Description of Investments: Stocks of and medium-sized U.S. company
        1. Money is invested in stocks for about 500 companies that represent U.S. stock market performance.
      2. Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Standard and Poor’s 500 (S&P 500) index.
      3. Risk: Market risk, Inflation risk.
      4. Volatility: Low to moderate Type of earnings: Change in market prices, Dividends
    5. S Fund
      1. Description of Investments: of small- to medium-sized U.S. companies (not included in the C Fund).
        1. Money is invested into: companies that have a market value greater the $1 billion.
        2. Infrequently traded stocks and those that sell for less than $1.00 per share are excluded from the S Fund.
        3. The S and C Funds combined, cover almost the entire U.S. stock market.
      2. Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Dow Jones U.S. Completion TSM Index.
      3. Risk: Market risk, Inflation risk.
      4. Volatility: Moderate to high Type of earnings: Change in market prices, Dividends
      5. The advantages and disadvantages of investing in the S and C Funds are about the same; except that stock prices for mid- and small-size companies are more volatile, making the S Fund the riskier investment of the two investment funds.
    6. I Fund
      1. Description of Investments: stock of 22 developed countries.
        1. The stocks represent well-known foreign companies such as Toyota Motor Corporation, British Petroleum, and Nestlé.
      2. Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Morgan Stanley Capital International EAFFE (Europe, Australia, Asia (Far East)) Index
      3. Risk: Market risk, Currency risk, Inflation risk.
        1. This fund has the risk of foreign currency fluctuations. If the value of the U.S. dollar decreases, the value of foreign stocks increase.
      4. Volatility: Moderate to high (Historically more volatile than the C Fund) Type of earnings: Change in market prices, change in relative value of currency. Dividends.
    7. L Fund
      1. Description of Investments: These funds are invested in the G, F, C, S, and I Funds.
        1. Funds are allocated based on 10-year increments (e.g., 2020, 2030, 2040, etc.) according to how long the member has until retirement.
      2. Objective of fund: To provide professionally diversified portfolios based on various time horizons using the G, F, C, S, and I Funds.
      3. Risk: Exposed to all of the types of risks to which the individual TSP funds are exposed – but total risk is reduced through diversification among the five individual funds.
      4. Volatility: Asset allocation shifts as time horizon approaches to reduce volatility. Type of earnings: Composite of earnings in the underlying funds.
    8. You can change your account balances if you wish, by requesting an inter-fund transfer using one of the following:
      1. http://www.tsp.gov/account/index.html (and pin#).
      2. Thrift line (1-877-968-3778)
      3. Investment Allocation Form (TSP-U-50)
