  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Definition and Purpose of the Thrift Savings Plan (TSP)
      • a.Definition -- An optional savings and investment plan open to military and federal civilian personnel.
      • b.Purpose -- Provides retirement income to supplement payments from the Navy and federal retirement systems.
    • 2.Characteristics of the Thrift Savings Plan
      • a.Offers participants the same type of savings and tax benefits that many corporations offer under 401(k) retirement plans.
      • b.TSP contributions and earnings.
        • (1)Traditional TSP contributions are not taxed until the funds are withdrawn.
        • (2)Roth TSP contributions are taxed and earnings are not taxed when the funds are withdrawn.
      • c.Payroll deduction must be used to contribute to TSP.
      • d.As of 2015, you may contribute a minimum of one percent of your base pay up to a maximum of $17,500 per year.
      • e.You may also contribute from:
        • (1)Incentive Pay
        • (2)Special Pay
        • (3)Bonuses
      • f.Existing retirement accounts may be transferred or rolled over into the TSP.
      • g.Reserves, who are Federal Civil Service employees may have both civilian and military accounts.
      • h.The Secretary of the Navy may designate personnel in critical ratings to receive matching contributions into TSP.
      • i.In-Service withdrawals are allowed for participants who can document financial hardship.
        • (1)Before age 59½, you will pay income tax and an early withdrawal penalty and income tax.
        • (2)For information on TSP withdrawals go to www.tsp.gov or call 1-877-968-3778.
      • j.Members can borrow money against their accounts.
        • (1)For information on TSP loans go to www.tsp.gov or call 1-877-968-3778.
      • k.Upon separation from the Navy, there are several options for your TSP account:
        • (1)Receive a single payment for your entire account.
        • (2)Receive monthly payments based on the amount accumulated and one’s life expectancy.
        • (3)Use your account balance to purchase a TSP annuity, which pays a monthly benefit for life.
        • (4)Transfer TSP funds to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA) or other qualified retirement plan.
        • (5)Leave money in the TSP to continue earning interest.
          • (a)No further contributions are allowed.
          • (b)Withdrawal of funds must begin no later than age 70½.
    • 3.Thrift Savings Plan Investment Funds and Their Characteristics
      • a.Thrift Savings Plan participants can invest into any of the following funds.
        • (1)G (U.S. Government)
        • (2)F (Fixed-income Index Investment)
        • (3)C (Common Stock Index)
        • (4)S (Small Capitalization Stock Index)
        • (5)I (International Stock Index)
        • (6)L (Lifecycle)
      • b.G Fund
        • (1)Description of Investments: Government securities.
        • (2)Objective of fund: Interest income without risk of loss of principal.
        • (3)Risk: Risk of inflation.
        • (4)Volatility: Low Type of earnings: Interest
        • (5)The G Fund provides a steady rate of return.
      • c.F Fund
        • (1)Description of Investments: Government, corporate, and mortgage-backed bonds.
        • (2)Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Barclays Capital U.S. Aggregate Bond Index.
        • (3)Risk: Market risk, Credit risk, Prepayment risk, Inflation risk.
        • (4)Volatility: Low to moderate Type of earnings: Change in market prices, Interest
      • d.C Fund
        • (1)Description of Investments: Stocks of large and medium-sized U.S. companies
          • (a)Money is invested in stocks for about 500 companies that represent overall U.S. stock market performance.
        • (2)Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Standard and Poor’s 500 (S&P 500) index.
        • (3)Risk: Market risk, Inflation risk.
        • (4)Volatility: Low to moderate Type of earnings: Change in market prices, Dividends.
      • e.S Fund
        • (1)Description of Investments: stocks of small- to medium-sized U.S. companies (not included in the C Fund).
          • (a)Money is invested into companies that have a market value greater than $1 billion.
          • (b)Infrequently traded stocks and those that sell for less than $1.00 per share are excluded from the S Fund.
          • (c)The S and C Funds combined, cover almost the entire U.S. stock market.
        • (2)Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Dow Jones U.S. Completion TSM Index.
        • (3)Risk: Market risk, Inflation risk.
        • (4)Volatility: Moderate to high Type of earnings: Change in market prices, Dividends
        • (5)The advantages and disadvantages of investing in the S and C Funds are about the same; except that stock prices for mid- and small-size companies are more volatile, making the S Fund the riskier investment of the two investment funds.
      • f.I Fund
        • (1)Description of Investments: International stock of 22 developed countries.
          • (a)The stocks represent well-known foreign companies such as Toyota Motor Corporation, British Petroleum, and Nestlé.
        • (2)Objective of fund: To match the performance of the Morgan Stanley Capital International EAFFE (Europe, Australia, Asia (Far East)) Index
        • (3)Risk: Market risk, Currency risk, Inflation risk.
          • (a)This fund has the risk of foreign currency fluctuations. If the value of the U.S. dollar decreases, the value of foreign stocks increase.
        • (4)Volatility: Moderate to high (Historically more volatile than the C Fund) Type of earnings: Change in market prices, change in relative value of currency. Dividends.
      • g.L Fund
        • (1)Description of Investments: These funds are invested in the G, F, C, S, and I Funds.
          • (a)Funds are allocated based on 10-year increments (e.g., 2020, 2030, 2040, etc.) according to how long the member has until retirement.
        • (2)Objective of fund: To provide professionally diversified portfolios based on various time horizons using the G, F, C, S, and I Funds.
        • (3)Risk: Exposed to all of the types of risks to which the individual TSP funds are exposed – but total risk is reduced through diversification among the five individual funds.
        • (4)Volatility: Asset allocation shifts as time horizon approaches to reduce volatility. Type of earnings: Composite of earnings in the underlying funds.
      • h.You can change your account balances if you wish, by requesting an inter-fund transfer using one of the following:
        • (1)http://www.tsp.gov/account/index.html (and pin#).
        • (2)Thrift line (1-877-968-3778)
        • (3)Investment Allocation Form (TSP-U-50)
