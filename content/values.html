  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Values and Their Influence on a Person’s Behavior
      • a.Values are the ideals, beliefs, customs, or principles that a person holds dear.
      • b.Throughout our lives, we learn values from:
        • (1)Family
        • (2)Friends
        • (3)Church
        • (4)School
        • (5)Community
        • (6)Country
      • c.The lawless behavior displayed in society today indicates that some people have a diminished value system.
    • 2.Definition of Navy Core Values
      • a.Throughout history, the Navy has successfully met all challenges with basic principles that were developed during its early days.
      • b.When we joined the Navy, we accepted the values associated with our service. These are the Navy Core Values:
        • (1)Honor -- I am accountable for my professional and personal behavior. I will be mindful of the privilege I have to serve my fellow Americans.
        • (2)Courage -- Courage is the value that gives me the moral and mental strength to do what is right, with confidence and resolution, even in the face of temptation or adversity.
        • (3)Commitment -- the day-to-day duty of every man and woman in the Department of the Navy is to join together as a team to improve the quality of our work, our people, and ourselves.
        • (1)
    • 3.Behaviors that Detract from the Navy Core Values
      • a.Hazing
        • (1)Any conduct performed without proper authority that causes another person to suffer or be exposed to an activity which is:
          • (a)Cruel
          • (b)Abusive
          • (c)Humiliating
          • (d)Oppressive
          • (e)Demeaning
          • (f)Harmful
      • b.Discrimination
        • (1)An act that denies equal treatment of people or groups based on race, color, sex (gender) , age, or religion etc.
      • c.Sexual Harassment
        • (1)A form of discrimination that involves UNWELCOME
          • (a)Sexual advances.
          • (b)Requests for sexual favors.
          • (c)Verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.
        • (2)The request for such conduct can be either direct or indirect.
        • (3)Negatively affects a person’s job or career.
      • d.Fraternization
        • (1)Inappropriate relationships between superiors and subordinates that could result in favoritism or discipline problems on the job.
      • e.Alcohol or drug abuse
        • (1)Misuse of alcohol or drugs could result in the inability to perform your job. causing others to pick up the slack.
      • f.Hazing, discrimination, sexual harassment, and fraternization are prohibited and violate the Navy Core Values.
        • (1)Honor
          • (a)By refusing to participate in these behaviors, you are:
            • 1)Maintain integrity.
            • 2)Conduct youself ethically.
          • (b)Personnel must have appropriate relationships with seniors and subordinates, Do NOT fraternize.
        • (2)Courage -- Despite peer pressure, personnel must have the courage to:
          • (a)Not participate in undesirable activities; e.g., Ku-Klux Klan events.
          • (b)Discourage others from participating in undesirable activities.
        • (3)Commitment -- Personnel must be committed to:
          • (a)Upholding the Navy human rights policies.
          • (b)Showing respect for other people.
    • 4.Putting Navy Core Values Into Action
      • a.Suicide takes shipmates from us too soon. Using Core Values we can possibly make a difference in someone’s life and save a shipmate.
      • b.Increasing awareness of suicide concerns, improving wellness, and esduring personnel know how to intervene when someone needs help.
        • (1)Ask - If you see a shipmate in trouble.
          • (a)It takes courage to intervene with a troubled shipmate.
        • (2)Care - Show concern and seek assistance.
          • (a)It takes commitment to show concern by listening without judging.
        • (3)Treat - Help get the professional assistance needed.
          • (a)It is honorable to assist shipmates by connecting them with appropriate professionals to assist with troubles.
    • 5.The Effects of Low Self-Esteem on Relationships with RDCs and Other Recruits
      • a.Low self-esteem can lead to inappropriate relationships because of the need for attention.
        • (1)RDC’s attention should not be misinterpreted as sexual interest. RDC are teachers and professionals, not friends.
        • (2)Think of yourself as a worthwhile person, a professional, and an honorable patriot serving your country.
      • b.It is not necessary to compromise your values to gain favor from RDCs and peers.
    • 6.Limitations of Gender Relationships Between Recruit and Recruit, and Recruit and RDCs During Recruit Training
      • a.Recruit training is not the place to look for romantic or sexual relationships.
        • (1)Neither serious nor casual interpersonal relationships with recruits should be pursued, even after graduation from recruit training, if still assigned to RTC.
        • (2)Friendships with RDCs are not permissible. Even after graduation, staff-to-former recruit relationships are not allowed.
      • b.During recruit training, consider other recruits as shipmates or family.
      • c.Over the course of eight weeks, some emotions can enter into the relationship between recruits and toward RDCs. Do NOT allow such emotions to go beyond professional boundaries.
      • d.In society, people may use their sexuality to get ahead (i.e., sexual politics). Such relationships do not last, have long-term consequences (e.g., health, reputation, financial, etc.), and could cause a breakup of an existing relationship.
      • e.The use of sexual politics by Navy personnel could result in:
        • (1)Punishment under the UCMJ.
        • (2)Denial of promotion.
    • 7.Goals of the Navy Core Values
      • a.Learn -- Know what the Navy Core Values are and how they contribute to the readiness of the Navy.
      • b.Adopt -- It is essential that you adopt Honor, Courage, and Commitment as your own to:
        • (1)Guide your behavior 24 hours a day.
        • (2)Use in your professional and personal life.
      • c.Think
        • (1)Recognize when Core Values are being compromised. Compromised values lead to:
          • (a)Hazing, discrimination, harassment, violence, and theft.
          • (b)Making bad choices based on fear of:
            • 1)Reprisal from peers or seniors.
            • 2)Getting someone else in trouble.
        • (2)Place Honor, Courage, and Commitment in the forefront of your life to:
          • (a)Increases your credibility.
          • (b)Influence others.
          • (c)Improve your personal and command effectiveness.
      • d.Act -- Do the right thing.
        • (1)Apply the Navy Core Values in your dealings with shipmates and seniors.
        • (2)Support the Navy’s equal opportunity policies; have the courage to report violations, whether you are a witness or victim, to the chain of command:
          • (a)Verbally
          • (b)Using the Navy Equal Opportunity (EO) Formal Complaint Form NAVPERS 5354/2. (While at RTC, recruits use the local RTC Form.)
        • (3)Report illegal activities that you observe or know about; illegal activities violate the Navy Core Values.
          • (a)However, originators of false reports will be punished.
          • (b)The chaplain can provide advice about reporting violations of the Navy Core Values and the law.
            • 1)By law, conversations with the chaplain are confidential.
            • 2)If needed, the chaplain will accompany personnel to report violations.
      • e.Re-evaluate -- Periodically reflect on your behavior in terms of core values. If you fall short, consider how to improve.
    • 8.Relationship between Teamwork and Behavior According to the Navy Core Values
      • a.Sometimes we are on automatic and do not realize that we are operating in a moral or ethical gray area.
      • b.Have the courage to tell shipmates when they are violating the Navy Core Values and identify gray areas in their behavior in a spirit of supportiveness; but do not be confrontational or critical.
      • c.We are all both shipmates and teammates
