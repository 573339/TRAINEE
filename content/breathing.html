A. Introduction

Your ability to use an Emergency Breathing Device could be the difference between you remaining alive or dying when you are in a hot, smoke-filled, burning space. These devices will provide the user with oxygen/breathable air to allow time for escape from the affected space.

  2. Emergency Escape Breathing Device (EEBD)
    1. Purpose
      1. Provides about minutes of oxygen for personnel:
        1. To escape through a - or toxic atmosphere to the weather deck of a surface ship.
        2. Trapped in a smoke-filled or toxic atmosphere and awaiting rescue.
      2. Should NOT be used when fighting a fire -- does NOT provide enough protection.
    2. Ocenco M20.2 Emergency Escape Breathing Device (EEBD)
      1. Components
        1. Cylinder -- Stores oxygen.
        2. Gauge
          1. Indicates the amount of oxygen in the cylinder.
          2. When the gauge reads , the EEBD is ready for use.
        3. Mouthpiece -- Provides for flow of oxygen to the user.
        4. Nose clip -- Ensures the operator does NOT inhale smoke or toxic gasses.
        5. Face shield
          1. Protects user from or toxic gasses.
          2. Worn as needed.
        6. Neck strap -- When EEBD is worn, provides support.
        7. Accessories
          1. Overcase (orange) -- Provides protection when EEBD is stored. (Blue overcase indicates Training EEBD).
          2. Case
            1. Location inside the overcase.
            2. Two part container housing the EEBD.
            3. Has a lever for opening.
      2. Procedures for donning and activating.
        1. Remove the EEBD from overcase (orange).
        2. Lift yellow lever to open the cover (discard the cover).
        3. Remove the unit from the case by pulling the yellow neck strap upwards. Oxygen will begin to flow.
        4. Insert .
        5. Put on the nose clip.
        6. Breathe through mouth and escape.
        7. If needed, pull the face shield over the head and pull outward on the O-rings to tighten the face shield around the neck.
  3. Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus (SCBA)
    1. Purpose of SCBA
      1. Protects the wearer from lack of or a atmosphere.
      2. Provides the user with air.
      3. Can be worn as breathing protection when fighting fires.
    2. Purpose and characteristics of SCBA components
      1. Cylinder Assembly
        1. Depending on the type, when fully charged stores a or -minute supply of compressed air.
        2. Rechargeable.
        3. Cylinder Valve -- When open, allows airflow through the apparatus.
        4. Dual-Reading Pressure Indicator - Provides visual indication of air in the cylinder.
      2. Backframe and harness assembly
        1. Supports the SCBA on the user’s back.
        2. There are two adjustable shoulder straps and an adjustable waist strap with pads.
        3. Mask-Mounted Regulator holder.
      3. Pressure Reducer (First-Stage Regulator) - Reduces pressure of the air flowing from the cylinder into the breathing apparatus.
        1. Attached components
          1. Quick Charge Assembly.
          2. Remote Pressure Indicator
        2. Remote pressure indicator
          1. With the cylinder valve open, indicates air cylinder pressure.
          2. Located on the straps so the SCBA user can easily read the cylinder pressure.
          3. The green zone indicates sufficient pressure to begin operational use.
      4. Mask-Mounted Regulator ( -stage ) -- Maintains a positive pressure of breathable air in the facepiece at all times.
        1. Purge valve
          1. Situated at the inlet of the mask-mounted regulator.
          2. In case of - regulator failure, can be manually opened to override the regulator to provide constant flow of air to the facepiece.
          3. When securing the SCBA, can be opened to (purge) air from the system.
          4. Color-coded red.
        2. Air saver switch
          1. Located on of the mask-mounted regulator.
          2. Starts and stops airflow from the mask-mounted regulator into the facepiece.
        3. Alarm (Vibralert) -- and when the compressed air supply in the cylinder goes down to 20 to 25% of capacity or the pressure reducer malfunctions.
      5. Facepiece
        1. Comes in three sizes:
          1. Small ( ) .
          2. Large/standard ( ) -- Fists most personnel.
          3. Extra-large ( ).
        2. There are amplified voicemitters on each side of the facepiece for communicating with others.
        3. Straps hold the facepiece snug against the user’s face to form a seal.
    3. Component interrelationship
      1. Compressed ( ) air flows from the cylinder, through the pressure reducer, where the air pressure is reduced, through the mask-mounted regulator, and into the facepiece.
      2. Exhaled air flows from the facepiece into the atmosphere.
    4. Donning procedures
      1. Perform a predonning inspection by visually inspecting the SCBA for damage.
        1. Inspect backframe and harness assembly, and facepiece for obvious damage.
        2. Visually inspect assembly for physical damage such as dents, gouges, abrasions, cuts, loose fibers, etc...
        3. Ensure minimum 4,000 psig reading on dual-reading pressure indicator.
        4. Ensure cylinder is properly secured to backframe.
      2. Don firefighting ensemble or general quarters (GQ) anti-flash hood depending on mission.
        1. Place hood over head and pull face opening over head and chin so face opening in hood is around neck are completely closed.
        2. Don firefighter’s coverall or fire protection gear (FPG). Ensure front and collar tabs of hood are completely closed.
        3. Don firefighter’s boots.

          4. Lean forward and pull down on both side straps to tighten.
          5. Secure waist adjustment strap quick-release buckle.
          6. Simultaneously pull waist adjustment straps forward and outward to tighten.
          7. Readjust side straps to ensure most of SCBA weight is on hips.

      1. To stop the , depress and release the switch.

    6. SCBA safety precautions
      1. Do not use a defective SCBA; have it checked by a Damage Controlman.
      2. Do not wear eyeglasses under the SCBA facepiece. The temples of the eyewear will prevent an airtight seal. Wear instead.
      3. To ensure a tight seal, ensure your face is clear of excessive facial hair.
      4. If you become , nauseous, excessively fatigued, etc. while wearing an SCBA, immediately return to the .

      6. When removing an SCBA after fighting fires, keep your on because SCBA buckles and the facepiece will be very hot and could cause to your skin.
      7. When securing an SCBA, do not disconnect any fittings until the excess air pressure is vented from the system by opening the purge valve.
