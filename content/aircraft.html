A. Introduction

During your Navy career, you will encounter many different types of aircraft. The types of aircraft have evolved to meet the challenge of changing warfare and doctrine. This lesson is an introduction to a brief history and overview of the US Navy aircraft mission and weapon systems.

The following Naval Standards will be touched on in this lesson: H038, H239. NAVPERS 18068F, Naval Standards for E1 through E3 may be found in the Recruit Trainee Guide

B. Enabling Objective(s):

  • 8.1 RECOGNIZE a brief history of Naval Aviation.
  • 8.2 IDENTIFY the types of U.S. Navy aircraft, their mission and weapons capability.

  1. Introduction

  2. US Navy Aircraft
    3. NOTE: Naval Air Reserve squadrons also maintain and operate many different types of aircraft. Their missions are the same as their active duty counterpart.
    1. Most of today’s modern Navy aircraft are no longer designed for a single mission. The evolution of warfare and conflict areas requires a capability.
      1. (F/A) F/A-18 Super Hornet
        1. An all-weather aircraft designed for interdiction, close air support, air defense suppression, and day/night precision strike and capable of in-flight fueling.
          2. F/A-18 Super Hornet
        2. F/A -18 weapons; M61 A1/A2 Vulcan 20mm cannon, Sidewinder, Sparrow, Harpoon, Harm, and Maverick missiles. Laser guided and various general purpose bombs, mines and rockets.
      2. (P)
        1. P-3 Orion and P-8 Poseidon (replacing P-3)
          1. Mission includes maritime patrol, reconnaissance, anti-surface warfare and littoral operations.
          2. Weapons; torpedoes, depth charges, mines, Harpoon and Maverick missiles.
            3. FP-3 Orion
            P-8 Poseidon (Harpoon missile)
      3. (C)
        1. The C-130 Hercules is used for transporting personnel and cargo.
        2. The KC-130 is configured as an inflight fueling platform for other aircraft.
        3. The C2 Grayhound is normally used for Carrier Onboard Delivery (COD), used for transporting supplies and personnel form land to aircraft carriers. The C2 will began a phased replacement beginning in 2018 by the V-22 Osprey.
          4. C-130 releasing flares
          KC-130 fueling aircraft
          FC-2 COD delivering personnel/supplies
        4. Weapons; The C-130 and KC-130 are not normally armed, but can be configured to launch offensive missiles and defensive measures. The C2 is not armed.
      4. (E)
        1. EA-18G Growler
          1. The EA-18G Growler conducts electronic warfare to include electronic jamming, communication countermeasures and satellite communications.
          2. The EA-18G carries air to air missiles, AGM-88 missiles (used to destroy surface to air missiles) and the ALQ-00 jamming system.
            3. EA-18G Growler
        2. E-2 Hawkeye
          1. The E2 Hawkeye provides battle management for attack of enemy ground targets, close air support coordination, combat search and rescue control, airspace management, datalink and communication relay for both land and naval forces. The E2 does not carry offensive weapons.
            2. E2 Hawkeye
      5. (H)
        1. H-60
          1. The H-60 is a aircraft configured for a specific mission. However, all are capable of Search and Rescue (SAR), Special Warfare support, reconnaissance and Vertical Replenishment (VERTREP).
          2. SH-60 primary mission is anti-submarine warfare.
          3. HH-60 primary mission is search and rescue and special warfare support.
          4. MH-60 primary mission is vertical replenishment (VERTREP)
          5. The H-60 is capable of carrying; the GAU-21 .50 caliber machine gun, rockets, Hellfire missiles and ACM-119 anti-ship missile.
        2. MH-53E Sea Dragon
          1. The MH-53E Sea Dragon is used primarily for Airborne (AMCM), with secondary missions of vertical shipboard delivery and assault support.
          2. The MH-53E can be fitted with the GAU-21 .50 caliber machine gun and 7.62 mm mini-gun.
            3. MH-53E Sea Dragon
      6. The Future – Unmanned Aircraft
        1. MQ-4C Triton
          1. The Triton will provide real-time intelligence reconnaissance missions over ocean and coastal regions, continuous maritime surveillance, and search and rescue missions.
            2. MQ-4C Triton
        2. X-47B Unmanned Combat Air System (UCAS)
          1. The X-47B UCAS is a tailless strike fighter, designed to help the Navy explore unmanned carrier aviation.
        3. MQ-8C Fire Scout
          1. The Fire Scout is an unmanned helicopter intended for ship-based intelligence gathering.
