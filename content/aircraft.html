• A.Introduction
    • During your Navy career, you will encounter many different types of aircraft. Aircraft have evolved to meet the challenge of changing warfare and doctrine. This lesson is an introduction to a brief history and overview of the U.S. Naval aircraft mission and weapon systems.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • RECOGNIZE a brief history of Naval Aviation.
    IDENTIFY the types of U.S. Navy aircraft, their mission and weapons capability.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.U.S. Navy Aircraft
      • NOTE: Naval Air Reserve squadrons also maintain and operate many of the same types of aircraft that the active squadron do, with the same mission.
      • a.Most modern Navy aircraft are no longer designed for a single mission. The evolution of warfare and conflict areas requires a multi-role capability.
        • (1)Fighter/Attack (FA) FA-18 Super Hornet
          • (a)An all-weather fighter/attack aircraft designed for interdiction, close air support, air defense suppression, and day/night precision strike. It is capable to preform and receive in-flight fueling.
            • FA-18 E/F Super Hornet
          • (b)FA-18 weapons; M61 A1/A2 Vulcan 20mm cannon, Sidewinder, Sparrow, Harpoon, Harm, and Maverick missiles. Laser guided and various general purpose bombs, mines and rockets.
        • (2)Patrol (P)
          • (a)P-3 Orion and P-8 Poseidon
            • 1)Mission includes maritime patrol, reconnaissance, anti-surface warfare and littoral operations.
            • 2)Weapons; torpedoes, depth charges, mines, Harpoon and Maverick missiles.
            • 3)The P-8 will replace the P-3.
              • P-3 Orion
              P-8 Poseidon
        • (3)Cargo/Transport (C)
          • (a)The C-130 Hercules is used for transporting personnel and cargo.
          • (b)The KC-130 is configured as an inflight fueling platform for other aircraft.
          • (c)The C-2 Greyhound is normally used for Carrier Onboard Delivery (COD), used for transporting supplies and personnel from land to aircraft carriers. The C-2 will begin a phased replacement beginning in 2018 by the V-22 Osprey.
            • C-130 Hercules
            KC-130 Hercules
            C-2 Greyhound
          • (d)Weapons; The C-130 and KC-130 are not normally armed, but can be configured to launch offensive missiles and defensive measures. The C-2 is not armed.
        • (4)Special Electronics (E)
          • (a)EA-18G Growler
            • 1)The EA-18G Growler conducts electronic warfare including electronic jamming, communication countermeasures, and satellite communications.
            • 2)The EA-18G carries air-to-air missiles, AGM-88 missiles (used to destroy surface-to-air missiles) and the AN/ALQ-99 jamming system.
              • EA-18G Growler
          • (b)E-2 Hawkeye
            • 1)The E-2 Hawkeye provides battle management for attack of enemy ground targets, close air support coordination, combat search and rescue control, airspace management, datalink and communication relay for both land and naval forces. The E-2 does not carry offensive weapons.
              • E-2 Hawkeye
        • (5)Helicopter (H)
          • (a)H-60
            • 1)The H-60 is a multi-mission aircraft configured for a specific mission. However, all are capable of Search and Rescue (SAR), Special Warfare support, reconnaissance, and Vertical Replenishment (VERTREP).
            • 2)SH-60 primary mission is anti-submarine warfare.
            • 3)HH-60 primary mission is search and rescue and special warfare support.
            • 4)MH-60 primary mission is vertical replenishment (VERTREP)
            • 5)The H-60 is capable of carrying the GAU-21 .50 caliber machine gun, rockets, Hellfire missiles and ACM-119 anti-ship missile.
              • SH-60 Seahawk
              HH-60 Seahawk
              MH-60 Knighthawk
          • (b)MH-53E Sea Dragon
            • 1)The MH-53E Sea Dragon is used primarily for Airborne Mine Countermeasures (AMCM), with secondary missions of vertical shipboard delivery and assault support.
            • 2)The MH-53E can be fitted with the GAU-21 .50 caliber machine gun and 7.62 mm mini-gun.
              • MH-53E Sea Dragon
