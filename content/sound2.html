A. Introduction

This lesson provides the basic information about lines and wire ropes that you will need for the basic seamanship laboratories. During your Navy career, you will be working or riding in small boats. This lesson discusses the types of small boats used by the Navy and the safety precautions for small boat passengers. In addition, this lesson will also teach you about sound-powered telephones. They are one of the most commonly used communication devices aboard ship.

The following Naval Standards will be touched on in this lesson: B276, G034, G035, G 036, G292, I310, I320. NAVPERS 18068F, Naval Standards for E1 through E3 may be found in the Recruit Trainee Guide

  4. Procedures to Secure Equipment for Sea.
    • When going to sea, you will need to secure equipment to prevent damage from rough waters. How equipment is secured depends on the gear and the place of stowage.
      • Use line string enough to hold the equipment. Make sure the line is in good condition.
      • Belay the objects from at least points that are near the object.
      • Secure all objects tightly against something (such as a bulkhead).
      • Use gear on sharp corners and rough surfaces.
      • Frequently check all secured lines and tighten as necessary.
    • SAFETY NOTES
      • Never secure a line to electrical cables, small pipes, electric motors, lifeline stanchions or anything not solidly secured.
      • Never block access to vents, fire plugs, switches, valves, doors or hatches.
