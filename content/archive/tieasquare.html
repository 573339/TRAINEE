A. INTRODUCTION

Except among seamen, the word knot is ordinarily used as an all-inclusive term. Even seaman find it hard to clearly define the terms knot, bend and hitch. We will be tying a square knot, bowline and clove hitch.

Knots are used to form eyes or to secure a cord or line around an object, such as a package. In other words, the line is bent around itself.

Hitches are used to bend a line to or around an object, such as a ring, spar, bollard or stanchion.

E. JOB STEPS

Distribute one piece of small stuff to each student and have student refer to the Knot Tying Wall Chart mounted in the classroom.

SQUARE KNOT

  1. Demonstrate the “Left over Right,” “Right over Left,” method while having recruits follow each step. Inspect all knots for correctness.
  2. Display slide “Square Knot” and let it continue to play as recruits’ practice tying the Square Knot a few times.

BOWLINE

  1. Display slide “Tying a Bowline” (Story of the Nervous Rabbit)
  2. Demonstrate how to tie a bowline by using “rabbit, hole and tree” method. Have recruits use their chair to tie the bowline and follow each step. Inspect all knots for correctness.
  3. Display slide “Bowline” and let it continue to play as recruits’ practice tying the Bowline a few times.

CLOVE HITCH

  1. Explain the terms “Bitter End” and “Standing End.” Show slide “Bitter and Standing Ends.”
  2. Demonstrate how to tie a clove hitch. Have recruits use their chair to tie the clove hitch as they follow along. Inspect all knots for correctness.
    1. Take a turn with the bitter end.
    2. Pass the end across the standing part.
    3. Take another turn. (notice both turns go around in the same direction)
    4. Pass the end under itself, and the hitch is complete.
  3. Display slide “Clove Hitch” and let it continue to play as recruits’ practice tying the Clove Hitch a few times.

BOWLINE ON A BIGHT

  1. Take a bight anywhere in the line ensuring it is large enough to tie the bowline.
  2. Using the bight as the running part, make a double strand overhand loop. (Same as the bowline)
  3. Take the double running part under the double overhand loop. (rabbit hole)
  4. Take the double running part through the “hole” of the overhand loop.
  5. Spread the eye of the running part (bight), pulling the bight through the overhead loop and pass it over the double strand bight.
  6. When the eye of the running part (bight) is around the standing part, grasp the double loop bight and the standing part. Pull the knot tight.
  7. Let the recruits’ practice tying the Bowline on a Bight a few times.
