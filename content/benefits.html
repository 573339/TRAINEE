  1. Introduction
  2. Education Benefits and Opportunities
    1. The Navy offers you many options to continue your education that will be beneficial to both your Navy and civilian careers when you complete your service.
    2. Off duty education
      1. Navy College Office
        1. Located at many facilities around the world to assist military personnel in coordinating off-duty education.
        2. Includes educational activities ranging from basic education skills to graduate studies.
          1. completion/General Equivalency Diploma (GED).
          2. Navy College Program for Afloat College Education (NCPACE).
          3. Defense Activity for Nontraditional Education Support . A self-study and testing program for high school, college and vocational class credits.
      2. Financial Assistance
        1. Post 9/11 / Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB)
          1. Provides financial assistance to obtain higher education or licenses.
          2. For members on duty and veterans with an honorable discharge.
        2. Reserve Montgomery GI Bill
          1. Must have six-year obligation in Selective Reserve.
          2. Must be a satisfactory participant in the Selective Reserve.
          3. Complete Initial Active Duty for Training (IATD).
          4. Must have a High School diploma or equivalency degree.
          5. Cannot receive financial assistance through an NROTC scholarship.
        3. Tuition Assistance (TA)
          1. Provides money to attend education voluntary during off duty hours.
          2. Pays up to 100% of tuition and expenses.
  3. Leave and Liberty
    1. Leave
      1. Definition - Authorized absence from one’s place of duty for an extended period of time, with pay and allowances.
      2. Types of leave
        1. Earned Leave - Leave you have accumulated (“on the books”). Can be used as:
          1. Leave - Military vacation time.
          2. Emergency Leave
            1. Granted for grave illness, serious injury or a death in the members immediate family: i.e., individuals listed on page 2 of your Enlisted Service Record.
            2. Maximum of days may be granted.
        2. Advanced Leave
          1. Leave you will earn in the future
          2. Using advanced leave will give you a negative leave balance.
        3. Convalescent Leave
          1. Used while under medical care: e.g., recovering after hospitalization.
          2. Must be authorized by the .
          3. Not charged against earned leave.
      3. Earning Leave
        1. Sailors earn 2.5 days per month, totaling 30 days per year.
        2. Members earn leave from their first day of active duty.
        3. Members are authorized to carry over a maximum of 60 days from one fiscal year (Oct 1 to Sep 30) to the next.
      4. Procedures for requesting leave
        1. Fill out an electronic Leave Request in the Navy Integrated Personnel System (NSIPS) (https://nsips.nmci.navy.mil).
        2. Forward Leave Request to your immediate supervisor for routing and approval.
          1. The CO or authorized representative gives final approval.
          2. The CO may disapprove a leave request because of:
            1. Operational/Training workload
            2. Previous abuse of leave privileges
        3. Leave granted in conjunction with transfer orders normally does not require submission of a leave request.
    2. Liberty
      1. Definition - An authorized absence from one’s place of duty for a short period of time that is not charged as leave.
      2. Types of liberty:
        1. Regular - Granted from the end of working hours to the beginning of the next workday; for example, weekend (48-hour) liberty will start after work on Friday and end at the start of the workday on Monday.
        2. Special - Granted during normal working hours. Can be;
          1. A reward for superior work performance.
          2. Compensation for long working hours.
      3. Procedures for requesting special liberty.
        1. Fill out a special request chit and route it through your chain of command.
        2. The may disapprove a liberty request because of:
          1. An operational/training workload does not permit.
          2. Abuse of leave privileges.
      4. Recall of Liberty - Liberty is subject to immediate recall.
  4. Other Benefits Provided by a Navy Career
    1. Active Duty Sailors
      1. Commissary and Exchange.
        1. The commissary is the same as a grocery store, prices may be reduced when compared to civilian grocery stores.
        2. The exchange is comparable to a department store. Example: Target, JC Penny, Best Buy. There are no State or Federal sales taxes on purchases in the Exchange.
      2. Retirement
        1. At the completion of years of active service, personnel are eligible for release to inactive duty and transfer to the fleet reserve.
          1. You are under the High 3 retirement system. At your 15 year point, you have the option to choose either the High 3 retirement system or the Career Status Bonus/REDUX (CSB) retirement system.
            1. High 3 retirement system provides a 50% retainer for 20 years of service based on your last 3 years. You earn an additional 2.5% for each additional year served to a maximum of 75% at 30 years of service.
            2. Career Status Bonus/REDUX (CSB) retirement system. You get a $30,000 taxable bonus, and your retainer is reduced to 40%. You receive a 1% COLA each year and 75% at 30 years.
        2. Service members who retire to the fleet reserve (20-30 years active duty) can be recalled to active duty at their previous pay grade.
        3. After thirty years of combined active service and inactive duty in the Fleet Reserve, personnel are transferred to the Navy’s retired list; they are only subject to recall to active duty at the discretion of the Secretary of the Navy in time of:
          1. War
          2. A national emergency
    2. Space-Available (Space-A) travel
      1. Free travel for military personnel and their dependents is permitted aboard DoD aircraft on a space-available basis.
        1. Space-A seats are made available only after all official duty passengers have been accommodated.
        2. Dependents are eligible only for flights that include travel outside the continental U.S.
      2. Personnel taking Space-A flights should have enough funds to purchase a return commercial airline ticket, because return military flights are not guaranteed.
  5. Medical/Dental Benefits
    1. You must be registered in the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System to receive Medical or Dental benefits.
      1. Service members and family members/dependents will be enrolled in Tricare Prime or another Tricare plan in certain situations. (remote duty, overseas duty, remote overseas duty).
      2. You are enrolled during In-processing.
      3. You also verify/enter family members such as spouse, dependent children/parents during your DEERS enrollment/verification.
      4. You should update DEERS through the local ID card office at your ship or shore command when any of the following occur.
        1. Retiring or separating from active duty.
        2. Getting married or divorced.
        3. Having a baby.
        4. Death of a family member listed in DEERS.
    2. Active Duty
      1. Active duty service members have no out of pocket costs for medical, vision and dental care.
    3. Active duty family members have no out of pocket medical care costs as long as the care is received from the assigned Primary care Manager (PCM) or with a referral.
      1. Must be enrolled in DEERS.
    4. Dental
      1. Active duty family members monthly premiums are based on military members status.
    5. Retirement
      1. Retired service members and their families are eligible for medical and dental services. A monthly enrollment fee is required.
    6. Veterans Administration (VA) benefits.
      1. Service members who served in the military and either retired or were discharged under any condition other than dishonorable may be eligible for medical/vision/dental services depending upon their personal situation.
  6. Relationship between The Navy Core Values and Military Benefits
    1. Honor - Be honest in using your benefits.
    2. Courage - To make good decisions.
    3. Commitment - Be committed to obtaining the most out of your benefits for both yourself and your family.
