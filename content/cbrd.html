• A.Introduction
    • In this lesson, you will wear an M50 gas mask in an atmosphere of chemical warfare gas. You will gain confidence that your mask will protect you in a hostile environment.
  • B.Enabling Objective(s):
    • IDENTIFY the purpose and characteristics of Mission Oriented Protective Posture (MOPP) levels as they apply to individual personal protective equipment.
    EXPLAIN the procedures for donning an advanced chemical protective garment (ACPG) with accessories and an M50 chemical biological mask.
    USE an M50 chemical biological mask for protection when entering a space flooded with tear gas.
    EXPLAIN the relationship between teamwork and donning chemical biological protective equipment.
    EXPLAIN the relationship between the Navy Core Values and participating in the chemical, biological, and radiological defense laboratory.
  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Purpose and Characteristics of Mission Oriented Protective Posture (MOPP) Levels
      • a.Purpose -- Establish shipboard levels of readiness for chemical/biological agent attack.
      • b.Levels
        • (1)MOPP Level 1 (Suspected) -- CBRD protective gear is issued to personnel.
        • (2)MOPP Level 2 (Possible) -- Protective mask is in carrier and worn by all personnel.
        • (3)MOPP Level 3 (Probable) -- Install a new canister on mask; don suit (including rubber boots) with hood down.
        • (4)MOPP Level 4 (Imminent) -- Don mask, hood, and gloves.
    • 2.Donning a Joint Service Lightweight Integrated Suit Technology (JSLIST) Overgarment with Accessories and an M50 Chemical Biological Mask
      • a.Pre-donning procedures
        • (1)Button shirt.
        • (2)Tuck trousers into socks.
      • b.JSLIST Overgarment (Coat/Trousers)
        • (1)Unpack coat and trousers.
        • (2)Check for damage such as punctures rips and tears.
        • (3)Donning procedures
          • (a)Trousers
            • 1)Point toes, put leg into trousers and pull trousers up: repeat for other leg.
            • 2)Close fastener and fasten two fly opening snaps.
            • 3)Pull suspenders over shoulders.
              • a)Fasten snap couplers
              • b)Adjust so trousers fit comfortably into the inseam.
              • c)Adjust waistband hook and pile fastener tapes for snug fit.
          • (b)Coat
            • 1)Don coat, close the slide fastener up as far as the chest.
            • 2)Secure front closure flap hook and pile fastener tape as far as the chest.
            • 3)Pull the bottom of the coat down over the trousers.
            • 4)Pull ends of waist elastic coat retention cord through the front of the coat to get a secure fit around the waist and tie the ends into a bow.
            • 5)Pass loose ends through the suspender loops on the front of the trousers from inside to outside.
      • c.Chemical protective footwear covers (CPFCs) (fishtail boots)
        • (1)Attach laces to the toes; ensure laces are even.
        • (2)Put on over your shoes (heel to heel).
        • (3)Pass laces through the rubber grommets on sides of boots.
        • (4)Cross laces over instep and through rubber grommets behind ankles.
        • (5)Cross laces over instep, wrap around ankle “roman sandal” style, and tie in front using a bowknot.
        • (6)Kneel, pull pant legs down over boots, and secure with hook and pile fasteners.
      • d.M50 mask
        • (1)Install new M61 Filters on mask..
          • (a)M61 filters are attached using a twist lock mechanism.
          • (b)When the filter is attached to the filter mount, it opens the self-sealing disk valve permitting filtered air to pass through the inlet valve into the mask during inhalation.
        • (2)Pick up mask; loosen straps and place them over and in front of the facepiece. (This is normally completed during MOPP I.)
        • (3)Remove your headgear.
          • WARNING: Before donning and adjusting a mask, remove earrings, hair fasteners, hair knots, buns, or braids that will interfere with the mask seal and let hair hang freely. Facial hair could result in an improper mask fit resulting in illness or death.
        • (4)Remove eyeglasses, contact lenses, and false teeth (if applicable) and place in right front pocket.
          • WARNING: DO NOT wear contact lenses (soft or hard) while wearing the masks. Inadequate oxygen supply to the corneal surface, exposure to dust, and smoke or gas may cause serious vision loss or eye damage. Personnel requiring vision correction are provided optical inserts for their protective masks.
        • (5)Fit mask under the chin.
        • (6)Using the head harness tab, bring the harness over the back of your head.
          • (a)Be sure your ears are between the temple straps and cheek straps.
            • NOTE: Brow and temple straps normally have been adjusted during initial fitting.
          • (b)Pull down on the head harness at the back as far as possible so the brow straps are tight and temple straps are approximately parallel to the ground.
            • WARNING: Once the mask is donned, do not pull on the brow or temple straps. Brow and temple straps have been adjusted during initial fitting.
        • (7)Tighten the cheek straps one at a time or both at the same time, ensuring straps lay flat against your head.
          • WARNING: Keep cheek straps tight while wearing the mask. If loosened the mask may lose its seal.
        • (8)Clear mask by placing your hand over the outlet valve cover assembly. Blow hard to ensure that any contaminated air is forced out around the edges of the mask.
        • (9)Test the mask for proper seal.
          • (a)Placing the palm of your hands over the filter inlet passages.
          • (b)Gently inhale until the mask collapses to ensure no damage occurs to the exhalation valve.
        • (10)Hold your breath for ten (10) seconds; if the mask remains collapsed, you have a proper seal.
        • (11)If your mask does NOT remain collapsed during the ten seconds, readjust and retest.
        • (12)When the mask properly seals, breathe normally.
      • e.Hood and protective gloves
        • (1)Pull hood over head and mask.
        • (2)Close slide fastener on the coat front, secure the flap with hook and pile fastener.
        • (3)Place edge of garment hood around edge of the mask. Secure the flap above the fastener with hook and pile fastener.
        • (4)Snap barrel locks together under chin. Slide barrel locks up to hood to maintain seal.
        • (5)Have another person check hood seal around mask to ensure hood is positioned properly and skin is not exposed.
        • (6)Pull sleeves up arm; don white cotton liner and black rubber outer chemical protective gloves.
        • (7)Pull sleeves down over your gloves, and secure with the hook and pile fasteners.
    • 3.Relationship between Teamwork and Donning Chemical-Biological Protective Equipment -- Use teamwork when donning protective equipment by checking each other to ensure:
      • a.All skin areas are covered.
      • b.The JSLIST hood is positioned properly.
    • 4.Relationship between the Navy Core Values and Participating in the Chemical, Biological, and Radiological Defense Laboratory
      • a.Courage -- All students will have to display Courage to enter the CBRD chamber, remove their masks, and state their name, rank, and division number.
      • b.Commitment -- Be Committed to completing the evolution in spite of the consequences.
