OS 2-14-1 Outline Sheet

A. Introduction

B. Enabling Objective(s):

  • 66.1 IDENTIFY the difference between standard Military Transfer Orders and Temporary Assignment for Duty Orders.
  • 66.2 DESCRIBE the contents of the Header Section of standard Military Transfer Orders.
  • 66.3 DESCRIBE the contents of Part One of standard Military Transfer Orders.
  • 66.4 DESCRIBE the contents of Part Two of standard Military Transfer Orders.
  • 66.5 DESCRIBE the contents and proper handling of Military Transfer Packages.

C. Topic Outline:

  1. Introduction
  2. Official Orders
    1. Military Transfer Orders
      1. Generated by Navy Personnel Command (NAVPERSCOM) or Enlisted Personnel Management Command (EPMAC).
      2. Provide specific information needed for smooth transition from one duty assignment to another.
      3. Received in time to make transfer arrangements.
      4. Provide accounting data used for Navy personal accounting.
      5. Orders may be in several formats but all contain the same general information.
    2. Temporary Assignment for Duty (TAD) Orders
      1. Generated by local Commands.
  3. Military Transfer Orders Header
    1. From
    2. Subject
  4. Military Transfer Orders Part One
    1. Fiscal Year (FY) Funding notice
    2. Detaching Activity
      1. When you are leaving.
      2. Command presently attached to.
      3. Duty Type.
    3. Intermediate Activity(s) (If any)
      1. When you are to report.
      2. Who you are reporting to.
      3. Where you are to report.
      4. Why you are reporting.
      5. How long you can expect to be there.
      6. What to do when you leave there.
    4. Ultimate Duty Station
      1. When you are to report.
      2. Who you are reporting to.
      3. Where you are to report.
      4. Why you are reporting.
      5. Assigned Rate
        1. Detailed NEC
        2. Projected Rotation Date (YYMM)
      6. Supporting PSD
      7. Sponsor Information
      8. Accounting Data
  5. Military Transfer Orders Part Two
    1. Orders ID
    2. Information for you prior to detaching
      1. Medical care while in transit.
      2. Exceptional Family Member (EFM) guidance
      3. Travel information details.
      4. Obligated service requirements.
      5. HIV test requirements.
      6. Actions required if reporting to/transferring from a deployed unit.
      7. Housing needs information.
      8. Overseas screening requirements.
      9. Where to go to get information on your next duty station.
      10. Lodging information.
      11. Transportation needs.
  6. Travel Package
    1. Contents
      1. Inside
        1. Medical Record
        2. Dental Record
        3. Copies of your orders
        4. Copies of any screening packages you need.
      2. Outside
        1. Copy of your orders
        2. Your travel itinerary
        3. Your travel ticket
    2. Your responsibility
      1. Review your orders
        1. Know what they are telling you to do and when.
      2. Review your Travel Itinerary
        1. Know where your travel itinerary is telling you to be and when.
      3. Control your travel package
        1. Know where it is.
        2. Know who can see the orders or itinerary attached to it.
        3. Take care of it.
      4. Turn your orders in when you report aboard.
  7. Summary and Review
