    3. Chemical protective footwear covers (CPFCs) (fishtail boots)
      1. Attach to the toes; ensure laces are even.
      2. Put on over your shoes (heel to heel).
      3. Pass laces through the rubber grommets on sides of boots.
      4. Cross laces over instep and through rubber behind ankles.
      5. Cross laces over instep, around ankle “roman sandal” style, and tie in front using a bowknot.
      6. Kneel, pant legs down over boots, and secure with hook and pile fasteners.
    4. MCU-2/P mask
      1. Install new C-2 Filter on MCU-2/P mask.
        1. C-2 filters are attached using a twist lock mechanism.
        2. When the filter is attached to the filter mount, it opens the self-sealing disk valve permitting air into the mask during inhalation.
      2. Pick up mask; loosen straps and place them over and in front of the face piece.
      3. Hold your breath, to or death as if you were under chemical or biological attack.
      4. Remove your headgear.
      5. Remove eyeglasses, contact lenses, and false teeth (if applicable) and place in right front pocket.
      6. Fit mask under the .
      7. Using the head harness tab, bring the over the back of your head.
        1. Be sure your ears are between the temple straps and cheek straps.
        2. Pull down the head harness at the back as far as possible so the brow straps are tight and temple straps are approximately to the ground.
      8. Tighten the cheek straps, straps lay flat against your head.
      9. Clear mask by expelling the air in your lungs.
      10. Test the mask for proper seal by:
        1. Placing the of your hand over the filter opening.
        2. Inhaling until the mask . (Inhale gently to ensure no damage occurs to the exhalation valve.)
      11. Hold your breath for seconds; if the mask remains collapsed, you have a proper seal.
      12. If your mask does NOT remain collapsed during the ten seconds, readjust and retest.
      13. When the mask properly seals, breathe normally.
    5. Hood and protective gloves
      1. Pull hood over head and mask.
      2. Close slide fastener on the coat front, secure the flap with hook and pile fastener.
      3. Place edge of garment hood around edge of the mask. Secure the flap above the fastener with hook and pile fastener.
      4. Snap barrel locks together under chin. Slide barrel locks up to hood to maintain seal.
      5. Have another person check hood seal around mask to ensure hood is positioned properly and is not exposed.
      6. Pull sleeves up arm; don white cotton liner and black rubber outer chemical protective gloves.
      7. Pull sleeves down over your gloves, and secure with the hook and pile fasteners.
