This guide is to aid you throughout your learning process from recruit to Sailor. Each topic represents a lesson or lesson series. Use the Table of Contents to locate the lesson topics. Your Recruit pision Commander (RDC) will normally inform you of what topics to study. Utilize the pision daily schedule that is posted in your compartment to learn what classes are scheduled and the topics to study.

Six Kinds of Documents

Each topic has an Outline, and many topics include assignments as well. Only a few topics include Information, Job, Diagram, or Problem.

Outlines: Provide an brief overview of the major teaching points and follow the instructor’s lesson plan. Outline help you to follow the lesson as it is taught and provide places for you to write notes.

Assignments: Are study guides that help you prepare for lessons and laboratory/practical exercises. Complete and study the appropriate Assignment before you take an examination.

Information: Provide material from references, technical manuals, and books. Information sections serves as handy references for important topic material.

Jobs/Practical: Provide systematic instructions to help you learn and perform job tasks.

Enabling Objectives

You will see at least one Enabling Objective (EO) in each topic. Enabling Objectives identify the knowledge or skill you are to learn. You should try to understand the Enabling Objective(s) for each lesson topic while studying the topic’s Outline.

Study

One or more instructors will conduct classroom and laboratory sessions however some will be taught by your RDC. You are responsible for completing the material in this guide. Read the table of contents and the other front matter in this guide. This will help you become familiar with the organization of the material.

To Review a Topic: Turn to the topic’s Outline. Read the Enabling Objective(s) first, and then review the entire Outline.

To Study a Topic: Turn to the topic’s Study Assignment. Study the Introduction, then read the Assignments including the Bluejacket’s Manual. Finally, write the answers to the Study Questions.

Tests

You will take three written “Tests” while at Recruit Training Command. All material presented is testable. Success on the written tests is dependent upon understanding the objectives, paying attention in class, taking notes, and studying the material. In addition to Written Tests, “Performance Tests” are used to determine your skills in swimming, keeping your locker, military dress, military drill and applying concepts learned through the end of training.

Critiques

At various times during training you will have the opportunity to provide your feedback on facilities, services, staff, and training. Use these opportunities to provide us your input on what we can do to improve the learning experience at RTC.

Included at the back of this Trainee Guide are Feedback sheets to provide the curriculum staff your input on issues you encounter with the Trainee Guide. Please take the time to document issues and suggestions on how to improve this product. Following each academic test, the instructor will collect the feedback sheets. These sheets may provide insight into changes or corrections to improve the training for Sailors in the future.

Naval Standards

Naval standards are skills and knowledge, other than those defined by occupational standards, which are essential to the overall effectiveness of enlisted personnel in the performance of duty. They encompass military requirements; essential virtues of pride of service in support of oath of enlistment; maintenance of good order and discipline; and basic skills and knowledge pertaining to the well-being of Navy personnel, which directly contribute to the mission of the Navy.

Reference: NAVPERS 18068F, Navy Enlisted Manpower and Personnel Classifications and Occupational Standards, Volume I Navy Enlisted Classifications.
