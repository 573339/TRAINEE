  • C.Topic Outline
    • 1.Education and Training Benefits Provided by a Navy Career
      • a.Formal Navy schools
        • (1)Apprenticeship training
          • (a)Teaches personnel in pay grades E-1 through E-3 the basic shipboard skills and knowledge required for entry into groups of ratings.
          • (b)There are three apprenticeship schools.
            • 1)Airman
            • 2)Fireman
            • 3)Seaman
        • (2)“A” schools -- Provide basic technical knowledge and skills required for job performance in a Navy rating.
        • (3)“C” schools -- Provide advanced knowledge and skills to perform a particular job.
      • b.On-the-job training (OJT)
        • (1)Takes place during daily operation and maintenance situations.
        • (2)Most widely used form of training in the Navy.
        • (3)Formal or informal instruction pertaining to a specific job, skill, or task.
        • (4)Drill/practice sessions enhance efficiency and teamwork.
      • c.Professional military training correspondence courses
        • (1)Military requirements nonresident training courses.
          • (a)Used to prepare for advancement.
          • (b)self-study packages.
          • (c)Provide the basic knowledge required for members of the Navy and Navy Reserve.
          • (d)Based on minimum Naval standards.
          • (e)Examples:
            • 1)Basic Military Requirements
            • 2)Military Requirements for Petty Officers Third and Second Class
          • (f)Available on the Internet at https://www.courses.netc.navy.mil/.
        • (2)Professional requirements nonresident training courses
          • (a)Used to prepare for advancement.
          • (b)For E-3, courses cover a group of rates, for example:
            • 1)Airman
            • 2)Fireman
            • 3)Seaman
          • (c)Self-study packages that present information and professional knowledge for a specific rate, for example:
            • 1)Damage Controlman
            • 2)Hospital Corpsman
            • 3)Machinist’s Mate 3 & 2
    • 2.Factors That Affect a Person’s Advancement in Rate
      • a.Performance Evaluations
        • (1)Your key to success in the Navy is sustained superior performance.
        • (2)Your supervisor observes your performance and submits input to the chain of command.
        • (3)Evaluations are submitted periodically. They are based on a 5.0 grading scale and are retained in your service record throughout your Navy career.
        • (4)Personnel will be evaluated on the categories listed on their Evaluation Report and Counseling Record:
          • (a)Professional Knowledge -- Technical knowledge and practical application.
          • (b)Quality of Work -- Standard of work; the amount of supervision required to perform the job.
          • (c)Equal opportunity -- Contribution to:
            • 1)Command morale
            • 2)Unit cohesivness
            • 3)The command’s Equal Opportunity Program.
          • (d)Military bearing/character -- Appearance, conduct, physical fitness, adherence to Navy Core Value.
          • (e)Personal job accomplishment and initiative -- Taking responsibility for doing the job.
          • (f)Teamwork -- Contributions to team building.
          • (g)Leadership (optional for E-1 to E-3) -- Organizing, motivating, and developing others to accomplish goals.
      • b.Navy-wide advancement examination and final multiple score
        • (1)Navy-wide advancement examinations are given to all advancement candidates, E-4 through E-7, who are:
          • (a)Fully qualified for advancement.
          • (b)Within physical readiness/body fat standards.
          • (c)Recommended for advancement by their Commanding Officer.
            • 1)Most important eligibility requirement.
            • 2)A recommendation is not automatic; it must be earned. The chain of command’s input is vital in obtaining CO’s recommendation.
        • (2)Exams are given in March and September for petty officers 3rd class, 2nd class, and 1st class.
        • (3)Exam can NOT be taken while in “A” school.
        • (4)The final multiple score is used to rank all individuals who took the same rating exam. It is determined by:
          • (a)Performance mark average
          • (b)Examination score
          • (c)Time in rate/grade (amount of time spent in your current pay grade)
          • (d)Awards
          • (e)Previous examination performances; i.e.,
            • 1)Passed Not Advanced (PNA) points
            • 2)Passed the exam, but the final exam score is not high enough to be advanced in a particular rating.
            • 3)Extra points are awarded toward the next rating exam.
    • 3.Advancement Requirements for E-1 through E-4
      • a.E-1 to E-2
        • (1)Complete recruit training.
        • (2)Nine (9) months’ time in rate.
        • (3)Be recommended for advancement by CO.
      • b.E-2 to E-3
        • (1)Nine (9) months’ time in rate.
        • (2)Complete Basic Military Requirements Nonresident Training Course.
        • (3)Complete the professional non-resident training course (SN, FN, AN).
        • (4)Be recommended for advancement by CO.
      • c.E-3 to E-4
        • (1)Six (6) months’ time in rate.
        • (2)Complete Military Requirements for Petty Officer Third Class self-study package.
        • (3)Complete the applicable professional requirements nonresident training course.
        • (4)Be recommended for advancement.
        • (5)Pass Navy-wide advancement examination with a high enough final multiple score to authorize advancement.
      • d.Learning and Development Roadmap (LaDR)
        • (1)A sailor-focused tool that provides guidance along a career development continuum to enhance a sailor’s professional development.
        • (2)Your rating specific LaDR is developed around significant career points, targeting knowledge and skills you need to develop to successfully advance through your career.
        • (3)Rating specific LaDR is located in NKO and will be discussed further in your training pipeline.
    • 4.Career Navigator Program
      • a.The goal of the Career Navigator is to help sailors make informed decisions about their personal and professional career opportunities by placing the power of career management systems at their fingertips.
        • (1)Under Career navigator, all enlisted active component (AC) and reserve component (RC) career management policies, process and information technology (IT) support systems are organized into one overarching program.
      • b.The Career Navigator Program allows Sailors to explore and choose available career opportunities. It encompasses all policies and procedures for career events, such as:
        • (1)Initial rating choice,
        • (2)Reclassification during initial skills training. (if needed).
        • (3)Career Development Boards (CDBs)
        • (4)PACT designation
        • (5)Retirement
        • (6)Converting to another rating
        • (7)Transition between active and reserve components
        • (8)Billet assignments (orders negotiation)
        • (9)Electronic service record management
        • (10)Career opportunity feedback surveys
        • (11)Preparing for advancement
      • c.Career Navigator provides self-service capability so that Sailors will be able to monitor their own information, career opportunities, and applications.
      • d.Career waypoints is the IT system that supports key Sailor decisions about the next career choice. The enlisted career management processes in Career Waypoints include:
        • (1)Reenlistment
        • (2)Reclassification
        • (3)Professional Apprenticeship Career Track (PACT) designation.
        • (4)Conversion
        • (5)Transition between components.
      • e.A career viewpoint survey will be available in the Navy Integrated Personnel System (NSIPS) so that Sailors are provided direct feedback on their Navy opportunities at major decision points in their career.
      • f.For further information about the Career Navigator Program you can look at policy, information and training materials located on the Navy Personnel Command Website: http://www.public.navy.mil/bupers-npc/career/careercounseling/Pages/C-WAY.aspx or contact your Command Career Counselor.

        • (2)Do NOT cheat on advancement exams.
        • (3)Do NOT achieve advancement by taking unfair advantage of others.
      • b.Commitment -- Be committed to:
        • (1)Completing your advancement requirements.
