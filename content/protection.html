Shipboard Force Protection Conditions (FPCONS)


  1. FPCON NORMAL Applies when a general global threat of possible terrorist activity exists and warrants a routine security posture.
  2. FPCON ALPHA Declared when an increased general threat of possible terrorist activity is directed toward installations and personnel.
    1. Security measures must be capable of being maintained indefinitely.
    2. Shipboard watches and pier sentries are armed and equipped with communication devices.
    3. Access to ship and pier is limited to include vehicles and small boats.
  3. FPCON BRAVO Is declared when an increased or more predictable threat of terrorist activity exists.
    1. Security measures must be capable of being maintained for weeks without affecting operational capability.
    2. Maintain appropriate FPCON ALPHA measures.
    3. Be suspicious of strangers, unattended vehicles, abandoned packages, and unusual activity and notify the duty officer.
    4. Prepare warning tape in both English and the local language to warn small craft to remain clear of the ship.
    5. Place additional armed sentries on the superstructure and the quarterdeck, prepare armed boat crews, and place them on 15 minute alert.
    6. Maintain one brow for limited access to ship.
  4. FPCON CHARLIE Is declared when an incident occurs or intelligence is received indicating that some form of terrorist action against installations and personnel is likely.
    1. Implementation of this threat condition for more than a short period will probably create hardship for the ship and personnel.
    2. Maintain appropriate ALPHA and BRAVO conditions.
    3. Cancel liberty and prepare to get underway in one hourâ€™s notice.
    4. Establish machine gun positions and deploy shoulder fired surface-to-air missiles.
    5. Deploy armed boat crews and a helicopter to act as an observation/gun platform.
    6. Energize radar and sonar systems.
  5. FPCON DELTA Is declared when a terrorist attack has occurred in the immediate area intelligence has been received that indicates a terrorist action against a specific location or person is imminent.
    1. Maintain appropriate ALPHA, BRAVO, and CHARLIE conditions.
    2. Prepare to get underway.
    3. Post sentries with machine gun to cover helicopter-landing areas.
    4. Deploy grenade launchers to cover approaches to ship.
