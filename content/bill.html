C. Topic Outline:

  1. Introduction
    1. As a person new to the fleet, you will have to know basic deck terminology in order to communicate with others. Basic knowledge about the ship is a requirement for successful performance of your job. The information in this lesson will help you understand the new surroundings on your first ship.

      The following Naval Standards will be touched on in this lesson: B273, H039, I310, I320. NAVPERS 18068F, Naval Standards for E1 through E3 may be found in the Recruit Trainee Guide
  2. Purpose of the Post 9/11 GI Bill
    1. Provides financial assistance to active duty military personnel and veterans to pay for education and training provided by Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) approved:
      1. Colleges and universities
      2. Business, technical and vocational schools
      3. Apprentice and on-the-job training programs
      4. Correspondence courses
      5. Flight training
      6. Remedial, deficiency, or refresher training
      7. License or certification tests
      8. High Tech Training
    2. Provides entitlement for up to full-time school enrollment. (Example 9 months school with 3 months break would allow to complete your education.)
  3. MGIB Entitlement
    1. To receive MGIB funds, members must complete an honorable period of active duty service.
    2. Members receiving the Loan Repayment Program.
    3. The following personnel are INELIGIBLE for MGIB enrollment:
      1. Members with prior active duty service
      2. Naval reservists on active duty time only for training.
      3. U.S. Naval Academy and other service academy graduates.
      4. Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC) scholarship graduates who received scholarship of more than $3,400 per school year.
    4. Reservists enlisting under the National Call to Service (NCS) Program are not eligible for MGIB Program enrollment during Recruit Training. They may become eligible if permitted to extend or reenlist on active duty for at least 24 months after obligated NCS service.
  4. MGIB increased benefit option designed to increase monthly benefits under the MGIB if you qualify.
    1. Some service members may contribute up to an additional $600 to the GI Bill to receive increased monthly benefits.
  5. Montgomery GI Bill
    1. Under the MGIB, without additional benefits:
      1. Individuals completing an enlistment of three years or more:
        1. For trainees on active duty, payment is limited to reimbursement of tuition and fees for the training taken.
        2. Can receive payments/entitlements of up to $ per month
      2. The monthly benefit paid is based on the:
        1. Type of training received.
        2. Member's length of service/obligation.
        3. Booster and NCF benefits vary individually.
    2. Payment by direct deposit is recommended.
  6. Pay Deductions for the Montgomery GI Bill
    1. MGIB: participants will pay $1,200 at $100 per month for the first 12 full months of active duty.
    2. Payments into the MGIB are non-refundable.
  7. The purpose of the Post-9/11 GI Bill is to offer many eligible participants an to other educational benefit programs, such as the MGIB for active duty, the MGIB-SR (selective reserves) and the MGIB-REAP (Reserve Educational Assistance Program).
    1. Your decision on which program to use should depend on several factors, including the type of education or training you plan to take and the amount of benefits received under each program.
    2. This information is intended to provide you an overview of the program. Specific benefits you may receive will vary depending upon the type of school you plan to attend and the state in which it is located.
  8. Post-9/11 GI Bill Entitlement
    1. Qualifying active duty service
      1. Available with 90 days of active duty or more, beginning on for after 11 September 2001; or
      2. Discharge with a service-connected disability after 30 consecutive days of active duty.
      3. 100 percent of maximum benefit payable with 36 months of service.
        1. Pro-rated benefits based on qualifying active duty time.
      4. Honorable discharge
      5. You do not have to pay into the Post-9/11 GI Bill to become eligible.
    2. Exceptions to qualifying active duty service
      1. Loan Repayment Program: Qualifying start date follows completion of 3 years of active duty.
      2. The Post-9/11 GI Bill allows officers who graduated from service academies or received ROTC scholarships to qualify for the GI Bill benefit. However, time spent satisfying the ROTC/Service Academy active duty obligation does not count toward the active duty service necessary to qualify for the benefits.
      3. Accessions Training (BMT) and 'A' school training time is excluded if active duty service is less than .
    3. Tuition & Fees when pursuing a college degree
      1. Allows NET payment of all in-state tuition and fees at public institutions, including graduate and professional degrees AND
      2. Caps private & foreign school tuition and fees at $20,235.02/year (Oct 14). (See www. to update payment caps)
    4. Tuition & fees when pursing a non-college degree
      1. NET payment for in-state tuition or fees for public institutions.
      2. $20,235.02/year (Oct 14) for foreign & private schools.
      3. On the Job Training (OJT) pays prorated on time in the program.
      4. Flight training pays the actual net costs not to exceed $11,562/year. (Oct 14)
      5. Correspondence courses pays actual net costs not to exceed $9,828.43/year. (Oct 14)
  9. Navy College Fund (NCF)
    1. Used to recruit some high school graduates into designated Navy jobs (ratings) designated by the Navy as "critical" or "hard-to-fill".
    2. Must be enrolled in the MGIB program
      1. NOT all who are eligible will be guaranteed NCF; it's awarded by the classifier, not the recruiter.
  10. Comparison of Entitlements
    1. See Information Sheet 2-02-2 in your Trainee Guide.
  11. Carefully consider the type of education you wish to pursue to ensure you elect the correct program.
  12. Summary and Review
